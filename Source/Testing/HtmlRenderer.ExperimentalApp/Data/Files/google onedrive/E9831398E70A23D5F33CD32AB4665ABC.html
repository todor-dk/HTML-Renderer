File Sharing

 

WVU has a number of methods available for sharing files. Determining which to use in a given instance depends on factors such as the size of the file and how long it needs to be available, as well as who will access it and from where.

 
 

Overview

Students and teaching faculty have unlimited Google Drive space available through their MIX accounts that can be used to upload files to share with others.

Faculty and staff can use the WVU FileLocker system when needing to send and receive files that are too large for e-mail, and cloud-based storage is available through OneDrive for Business.

When common space is needed for multiple employees, network drive space is an option, and when employee work groups need advanced collaborative resources in addition to file space, SharePoint can be an excellent solution.

Lastly, physical media, such as optical discs, flash drives, and portable hard drives can be options for transporting files when information security best practices are followed.

Google Drive


Teaching faculty and students have unlimited cloud storage where files can be saved and shared with others. This is the best place for faculty to share files with students, but the files can be shared with anyone having an email account. Access the space by logging into https://drive.google.com using your MIX email address and password.

Additional information is available on the Google Drive Support page.

OneDrive for Business

OneDrive for Business is a component of faculty and staff Office 365 accounts. It provides 1 terabyte of cloud storage space for files (soon to become unlimited), and is accessible from most any Internet-connected device. Collaborative sharing of files with colleagues is possible, whether or not they have Office 365 accounts.

While OneDrive for Business is very convenient and effective for some scenarios, it is suggested that teaching faculty use the Google Drive space within their MIX accounts to share files with students, and file sharing within university departments may be best done using SharePoint.

See http://it.wvu.edu/services/office365/onedrivebusiness for information on setting up and using OneDrive for Business.

Physical Media

CDs, DVDs, and removable drives are popular due to their portability and capacity, but risks exist for them to fall into the wrong hands. As such, proper security measures should be taken to prevent the contents from being accessed by unauthorized users.

At a minimum, encryption is recommended for files containing sensitive or Personally Identifiable Information (PII).

Please visit our Information Security Services site and the Acceptable Use of Data and Technology Resources Standard for more information on best practices and responsible use.

Filelocker

WVU users can immediately share files with anyone inside or outside the university using this simple system. It is an ideal solution when files are too large to send through traditional e-mail. Recipients receive e-mail notification of files being available, and follow links to download them.

Complete information about this service is available on the Filelocker information page.

SharePoint

SharePoint is accessible from anywhere, and allows for collaboration and development of documents, Wikis, discussions, blogs, and surveys. It is designed for situations in which work groups require regular access to shared resources, and need capabilities exceeding simple file sharing.

For more information on this service, please visit the SharePoint information page.

Network Drives

For departments needing access to a common storage area, space on the WVU Active Directory (WVU-AD) system can be a viable solution. This professionally managed space can be accessed by Windows and Mac systems from on campus, and through the MasterApps system when off campus. Those wishing to explore this opportunity should contact their departmental IT staff, or the ITS Service Desk.
