Which is better to use, OneDrive or Google Drive?

Benjamin Nelan, Freelance Motion Graphics Artist
Answered 7w ago

One of the reasons I use Google Drive is that I do all of my work out of my Gmail account. The way Drive blends into Gmail and offers large file attachments and media previews is super helpful.

In short, I use Google Drive because:

  • I'm already in the Google ecosystem.
  • You get 15 GB free storage (granted, that storage is shared between other Google services too), whereas OneDrive only gives you 5 GB.
  • The apps just work. The OneDrive app has crashed on me numerous times.
  • Google Drive's UI, both web and mobile, is really slick. It's all preference of course, but I'm not a fan of how OneDrive looks in comparison:

OneDrive (left), Google Drive (right)

Having said that, if you don't mind the interface and you have and use an Outlook account, OneDrive would probably be a way better option.

For starters, both offer additional storage for a fee,(the following is in Australian pricing):

  • Google Drive offers six paid plans, ranging from 100 GB at $25 per year up to 30 TB at $4,500 per year. Room to move!
  • OneDrive offers three paid plans, a 50GB storage-only plan at $24 per year (which they don't even have as an option on the mobile app by the way), a 1 TB at $89 per year and a 5 TB family plan at $119 per year.

But for comparison sakes, I'll take a closer look at two similar plans:

  • Google Drive: 1 TB, $125 per year
  • OneDrive: 1 TB, $89 per year

Not only is it cheaper to get 1 TB storage on OneDrive, you're also getting access to Microsoft Office and Skype Premium.

Even Adobe Creative Cloud, which is based around content creation, doesn't have 1 TB of storage included in the subscription. So Microsoft are offering a pretty great deal here in my opinion, especially with the family plan - even if it's not a deal I'm taking.

As I don't use Microsoft Office and only use Skype on occasion, the value in my case isn't there. I only recently started needing the 100 GB Google Drive plan for $25 a year - and for that price I'm getting double the storage than OneDrive offers at that price point.
