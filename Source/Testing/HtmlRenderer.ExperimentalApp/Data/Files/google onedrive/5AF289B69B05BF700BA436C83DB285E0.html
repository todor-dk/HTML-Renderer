Google Drive vs. OneDrive – With Free Online Storage is Best For You?

A cloud drive is undoubtedly one of the best things you can have on your computer. With free storage, free online backups that sync your documents instantly, and free tools like text editors, power point, and similar, drives are incredibly valuable. While there are dozens of options, Microsoft and Google have the two leading options in Google Drive and OneDrive.

Google Drive

Google Drive was actually the first drive to hit the market, and comes with a free word processor (Google Docs), free spreadsheets (Google Sheets), free drawing documents, free forms, printable maps, and file storage. A downloadable program allows you to access all of your folders in the drive on your computer, offline, and while you are connected to the web your files will sync in real time. You can also drag and drop files to your drive folder to automatically upload for you. Google Drive includes 16GB of free storage. A variety of free apps make it easy to upgrade and add features.

OneDrive

OneDrive is Microsoft’s version of drive and operates on a very similar basis to Google Drive. You install the program, and it automatically syncs everything in your Drive folder on your computer to your Drive in the cloud. Files upload automatically, and download automatically, so you won’t have to worry about backing anything up. OneDrive also includes a free version of Microsoft Word, Microsoft Excel, Sway, OneNote, and PowerPoint, which you would get from Office. If you have Office365, all of your files and documents from your online office automatically go into the cloud. 15 GB of storage allows you a lot of room for photos and videos or documents. You can also choose from a store full of free and paid apps.

OneDrive vs. Google Drive

If you’re looking for the most free things, Google Drive wins pretty much hands down. It’s also a little faster than OneDrive. If you prefer a clean, easy to use interface, you probably want OneDrive. Another consideration is that the text editing tools in OneDrive are significantly better than those in Google Drive, although both are comparable for general text editing and writing.Â Google Drive has the better app, while Microsoft’s app is clunky and very slow. For large file uploaders, OneDrive has a maximum file size of 10GB, and Google Drive has a maximum size of 5GB.

If you have Microsoft Word or Office 365, OneDrive is your logical option, because Office 365 automatically bumps your storage from 15GB to 2TB. Onedrive is also a little better at foler organization.

Essentially, Google Drive is the best choice if you want basic text editing, storage, and documents, but don’t care about the interface.

Microsoft’s OneDrive is a better choice if you want a cleaner interface, are very familiar with Microsoft Office, or want to upgrade to a paid subscription.
