Google Drive vs OneDrive: Which One is Better?

By Rahul Dasgupta — Last Updated: 30 Aug'16 2016-08-25T14:39:11+00:00 — Google+

For the past three years, I’ve been using both OneDrive and Google Drive for personal and work requirements.

But, I recently made a complete switch to Google Drive for all my storage needs.

One of the reasons for this switch is the fact that I’m not a big fan of Outlook, as I prefer Gmail. I also prefer Google Docs as a real-time document collaboration tool over Office Online.

Moreover, costs are always a major concern for me. Thus I like the various free offerings and cheap storage options Google Drive provides.

So, in this article, I’ll give a rundown of Google Drive versus OneDrive, and explain in more detail why I use Google Drive as my primary cloud service provider.

The Benefits Offered by Cloud Storage Services

 

Online Secondary Copies

I still remember my college days, when a presentation of mine got corrupted, due to a desktop virus which ended up infecting my USB drive.

I had to prepare the entire presentation, all over again, just one day before my final project submission date. A lot of hours and stress could have been saved if I had an online copy of that presentation.

This particular incident made me realize how important it is to use cloud storage services, to maintain a secondary copy of my computer’s files and folders.

Online storage services ensure that I’ve always got access to my data, even when the computer is down or dealing with a virus.

Sufficient Storage Space

One may argue that I should’ve used an email service to store an online copy of my corrupted presentation files during college.

If I had done so, the whole fiasco could’ve been avoided.

Well, in truth the files were considerably large, as they contained several images and video files.

So, sending such large files as attachments via email was impossible at the time.

Nowadays, I just upload a file to Google Drive and send the file’s link in an email, without worrying about how large or small an attachment is.

The best part about cloud storage is that, if the available space is no longer sufficient, you can always upgrade to a higher or unlimited storage plan.

Depending on the cloud provider that you use.

File Sharing and Syncing

Cloud storage solutions enable you to give friends and colleagues access to online files — in the form of links.

You can even determine the type of access that you wish to give, whether it is read-only or read-write access.

And, any changes made to files stored on your desktop will automatically sync with the copies of those files stored online.

Cloud providers also ensure that everyone with whom a file is shared with will have access to its updated version.

Real-Time Collaboration

The next time you’re sorting out an expense sharing sheet with friends, try using a cloud service tool.

It allows multiple users to view and edit the same document, online, at the same time.

And changes made by one user will not be overwritten by another.

Moreover, many cloud service platforms empower you to collaborate effectively, view changes as they’re made and track edits; to learn which user is responsible for a particular edit.

Real-time collaboration is a significant advantage to people working on files within a group or in team environment.

 

Things to Look For When Selecting a Cloud Storage Provider

 

Cost and performance are two critical parameters to consider when choosing a cloud storage service.

However, as many traditional online storage solutions offer excellent performance at competitive prices, it is necessary also to check out the below-mentioned parameters before plopping down cash.

File Versioning Support

It is important to choose a cloud backup provider who maintains multiple previous versions of a file stored online.

In this way, if anyone else with “edit” access to your data accidentally deletes it, the cloud service provider will help restore that file back to its previous version. 

So, remember to consider the number of file versions a cloud provider will offer to maintain, and for how long.

As you may have to revert to a previous version of the file, before it was infected by a virus or malware.

Customer Service

Just like every piece of software and tech, cloud technology, and its subsequent services are subject to bugs, issues, and other technical difficulties.

Which is why it’s important to confirm your cloud service of choice has a reliable customer support base.

And, no one wants to spend a lot of time getting such problems resolved themselves.

So, always look for a cloud provider that offers customer support round the clock, and responds quickly to any queries, doubts, and issues you may have.

Security

Using an online file storage and syncing service with great features doesn’t mean much without high security.

Hence, consider how well a cloud provider encrypts your data and manages authorization, to ensure safe storage and secure online access.

Also, you need to check whether the cloud provider offers data encryption, while it is in-transit or at-rest.

You can use zero-knowledge cloud providers, such as Sync.com, for data privacy and end-to-end encryption of the best order.

Also, SpiderOak is a good alternative for reliable security.

Welcome to the main event! I’ve used both OneDrive and Google Drive over the last few years.

While I’ve completely moved to Google Drive due to personal preferences, I still need to use OneDrive for clients who work with Microsoft Office tools.

Let’s compare the key features of these two highly popular cloud storage solutions — to determine a winner.

Customer Support

  • Google Drive

Google provides 24/7 customer support via:

  • Email
  • Telephone calls
  • Chat

With so many contact options available, you can choose what’s preferable, depending on the urgency of your issue or query.

Users can just leave their telephone numbers and wait for a quick call back from the support team.

I’ve received calls within two or three minutes, whenever I have used this method.

The call-back feature has helped me avoid the tedium of selecting options from a long menu over the phone.

You can also access Google Drive’s Help Center for guidance on how to use their services.

You can also get answers from other users on Google Drive’s Help Forum. It’s even possible to find answers to frequently asked questions by accessing the Known Issues Page.

  • OneDrive

Just like Google Drive, OneDrive offers round-the-clock customer support.

However, you can only get in touch with the OneDrive’s support team via email.

And that is it! There is no other way to ask them for help.Imagine facing a downtime issue which isn’t due to scheduled maintenance activities.

In such a situation, you can only hope OneDrive’s support team will respond quickly to your email.

Unfortunately, the support team does not respond quickly; I’ve experienced delays of 12 to 18 hours from their support team.

Also, I believe it is possible to explain certain issues much quicker and with better clarity over the phone, than in an email; but Microsoft does not offer a telephone support option.

You can access OneDrive’s Frequently Asked Questions section to get answers to common questions. And you can also visit the OneDrive Forum, to discuss issues or doubts with a user community.

Since OneDrive involves the use of several Microsoft Office products, you can also use Microsoft support.

Here, you can discuss issues or queries with an expert via chat, telephone call or by scheduling a callback.

Winner: Google Drive emerges the winner here due to its better all around support.

Security

  • Google Drive

Unauthorized access to data is a major concern for cloud users.

And, with Google Drive, you have to be extra careful, as all of Google’s offerings use the same login credentials.

However, Google now uses the secure HTTPS protocol for all its services.

Google also supports two-factor authentication to strictly ensure authorized access to accounts and data. It even uses SSL for encryption during transit between data centers and a computing device.

Moreover, while Google Drive does not support native encryption, it does encrypt data at rest by using 128-bit AES.

Data is most vulnerable to attacks when it is lying in one place for a long time, unencrypted. Hence, the at-rest-encryption feature offered by Google Drive is great.

  • OneDrive

Like Google, Microsoft ensures secure login credentials for multiple services, but it does not use HTTPS for all them. OneDrive also extends support for two-factor authentication, just like its primary rival.

And, while it does offer SSL encryption for data that’s in transit, OneDrive does not encrypt any data-at-rest.

This lack of encryption poses a huge security threat to data lying unprotected on OneDrive’s servers.

It is only possible to get encryption for data-at-rest with a OneDrive for Business account.

For business users, OneDrive offers this encryption on a per-file basis, where every file has a separate key. So, if one of the keys gets compromised, a hacker can only access that one particular file.

As part of an exception to its privacy policy, Microsoft has the right to scan your content and deem it as “objectionable” or “illegal” and delete it.

This exception makes me wonder how private data truly is from Microsoft’s team.

The exception was also a major reason for me using Google Drive instead of OneDrive, especially since one of my clients was apprehensive about such an exception to OneDrive’s privacy policy.

Winner: I prefer Google Drive because it ensures the encryption of data, even while it’s at rest, for all types of accounts or packages.

Collaboration

  • I believe that Docs is Google Drive’s trump card which provides us with excellent collaborative features. I have not personally come across a better online document collaboration tool, or one that’s any simpler or easier to use. With Google Docs, you can work with several users on the same: Document Spreadsheet Form Slideshow presentation Changes made by a user get reflected in the file, and you can even view modifications. Such collaborative editing saves a lot of time for teams and ensures you see changes in real-time, without delays. Google Docs also offers support for offline editing, which is quite a useful feature, that empowers users to work on files from anywhere and at any time. Of course, changes only sync when the file is connected once again to the internet.
  • OneDrive

Since 2010, OneDrive users have been able to use an online collaboration tool in the form of Office Online.

You can create:

  • Word documents
  • Powerpoint presentations
  • Excel spreadsheets
  • Other Office documents

From your OneDrive account.

For example, if you choose to create a Word document, OneDrive will open the file in Word Online.

It claims to offer real-time collaboration, but I’ve noticed considerable delays during editing, when more than one user is working on a document.

Also, you cannot make changes to a file using Office Online — without an internet connection. So, if you like to work with files on the go, even with an unstable internet connection, Office Online is not a good choice.

Of course, you can go for the paid version, called Office 365, and get appropriate real-time collaboration features.

However, why pay for a feature that’s free on Google Drive?

Winner: Since Office Online lacks offline editing and does not offer true real-time collaboration, my vote goes to Google Drive.

Storage and Pricing

  • Google Drive

Google Drive offers 15GB of free storage space for use across Gmail, Google Drive and Google Photos.

You can get additional storage space with different paid plans (not on an annual basis) for personal use.

The Drive for Business plans are as follows:

  • Google Apps for Work offers 30GB of storage and costs $5.00 per user per month or $50.00 per user annually.
  • Google Apps Unlimited offers unlimited storage space and Google Vault. This plan costs $10.00 per user per month.
  • Google Apps for Education is free for nonprofit K12 and higher education institution.

This last plan offers the same features, pricing, and storage space as the Google Apps for Unlimited package.

  • OneDrive

Although OneDrive used to offer 15GB of free space earlier, they now only offer 5GB. The current amount of storage space offered is quite a bit less than Google Drive’s.

If more space is needed, you can purchase any of the following OneDrive packages for individual use.

The OneDrive for Business and Office 365 plans are as follows:

  • Online Plan 1 offers 1TB of cloud storage space per user and costs $5.00 per user per month (on an annual basis).
  • Online Plan 2 offers 5TB of cloud storage space per user (for subscriptions with at least five users) and costs $10.00 per user per month (on an annual basis).
  • Office 365 Business Essentials offers 1TB of file storage space, a 50GB email box, and online versions of Office with email and video conferencing and costs $5.00 per user per month (on an annual basis).
  • Office 365 Business offers 1TB of file storage space, no email box, and fully installed Office on a Mac or PC with apps for tablets and phones and costs $8.25 per user per month (on an annual basis).
  • Office 365 Business Premium offers all the features of the Business and Business Essentials packages and costs $12.50 per user per month (on an annual basis).

Winner: Google Drive emerges as the obvious victor here, as its lowest paid plan offers 100GB of storage, compared to OneDrive’s cheapest plan, which only offers 50GB of storage.

Pros of Google Drive over OneDrive

  • Google Drive’s file versioning lasts 30 days, with a maximum of 100 revisions, while OneDrive justs keeps file versions for 30 changes
  • Google Drive offers quick real-time collaboration, with support for offline editing, while OneDrive can’t edit documents offline
  • Google Drive’s maximum file size is 5TB and 50GB for files converted to Google Docs format, OneDrive’s maximum file size limit is 10GB

Pros of OneDrive over Google Drive

  • OneDrive offers support for Windows tablets and phones, while Google Drive does not
  • You don’t need a OneDrive account to collaborate on different files, Google Drive requires a separate account for collaboration

In Summary…

Having compared the features of Google Drive vs. OneDrive, I have picked Google Drive as the clear winner.

Google Drive manages to satisfy most of the requirements of an excellent cloud service provider, featuring:

  • Quick real-time collaboration
  • Reliable customer support
  • Encryption for data-at-rest
  • Cheap prices
  • Large storage plans

Also, Google Drive offers more free storage space than OneDrive and even provides a plan with unlimited storage. Google Drive emerges as the more reliable and efficient cloud storage provider.

But, if your work revolves around Microsoft’s tools and devices, then OneDrive is still a good choice!
