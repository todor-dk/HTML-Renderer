How to sync Dropbox, Google Drive, and OneDrive account across devices with backups

By Ankita Gairola -
May 22, 2016
1284
0
Share
Tweet
Pin
+1

You can use desktop clients to easily sync your data across different devices with Dropbox, Google Drive, and OneDrive. Practically there are many ways to back up your necessary data or files by creating and storing copies on external hard disks, DVDs, CDs, pen drives, or memory cards.

But the simplest and faster method to do so is to upload files to the cloud. Doing so, you not only create backups of your entire files and data but you can also access the folders from any device, anywhere at your call.

The 3 most popular names– Google Drive, Dropbox, and OneDrive, offers online cloud storage for your files and folders. Here’s the one-by-one simplified method on how to sync your files with backups. It includes everything from sharing files with your friends to syncing data across all your supported devices.

Google Drive

Google Drive supports Windows 10, Android, iOS, and Mac OS X as well. It offers 15 GB for free. If you want more storage, you can go through different payment plans for monthly subscription. Once downloaded and installed,

  • Sign in with your Google account
  • See the tutorial at startup and learn how to add files to the Google Drive folder
  • Download the tool on your device/tablet, to auto-sync data, and share files with other people.

Drive also offers you to sync existing files from your online storage to your PC. You can change the folder location if it’s different from the default option.

  • Select sync only these folders option
  • Tick the boxes next to the directories you wish you copy on your PC
  • You can choose to sync everything in My Drive
  • You can also cancel this and later configure Sync option from the Preferences panel.

While syncing,

  • Click Google Drive’s icon from the taskbar notifications area to check progress
  • Open Google Drive folder or just visit Google Drive on the Web
  • Now click Open Google Drive folder to visit its location in Windows Explorer
  • You can copy items to the directory in Explorer to upload files to the cloud and sync them across different devices with Google Drive installed.

It’s just like performing a normal file copy task. Right-click on a selected file in Google Drive to share with others.

Dropbox

Dropbox supports Windows, OS X, Linux, Android, Blackberry OS, and Windows Phone. Dropbox offers 2GB for free, by default. You can buy more space by completing tasks or you can subscribe for 1TB/$9.99.

  • Sign in with your Dropbox or Google account
  • Click on open my Dropbox to view the tutorial and Get Started
  • Now click on the systray icon to open a menu with Recently Changed files
  • Copy link to share a file and let other Google or Dropbox users to download it
  • Click the globe icon to visit your online Dropbox account

Synced items will be marked green (as in Google Drive) whereas currently in sync will be marked blue. Right-click on a file and select Copy Dropbox link to share the item with other people.

OneDrive

OneDrive supports Mac OS X, Windows Phone, Windows, Android, iOS, and Blackberry. It offers 5 GB free storage and a few payment plans.

  • Sign in with your Microsoft account.
  • Go through the tutorial
  • Tick the box to sync files and folders in my OneDrive or to sync only these folders to your PC
  • Right-click the systray icon off OneDrive to open folder in Windows Explorer and share files across supported devices by copy/paste or drag & drop to this location
  • Select and right-click any file to share a Dropbox link with other people
  • Paste the URL stored in your clipboard to other friends to let them view and download the shared files.

Unlike Google Drive and Dropbox, OneDrive does not indicate synced or syncing files via any icons.
