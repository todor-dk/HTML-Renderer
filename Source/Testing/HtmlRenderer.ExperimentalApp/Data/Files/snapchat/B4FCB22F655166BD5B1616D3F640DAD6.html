How Snapchat Demographics Are Shifting

While Snapchat remains wildly popular with young social media users—over 60% of U.S. smartphone users are between the ages of 13 and 34 are Snapchatters—what’s more revealing is how Snapchat is now beginning to proliferate quickly among older demographics. As our Snapchat infographic illustrates, over 50% of new Snapchat users are over the age of 25, and the percentage growth of those aged 35 years and older is actually greater than the penetration rate for 18- to 24-year-olds.

Related Post: The 10 Snapchat Statistics You Must See To Believe

How Brands Can Capitalize On Snapchat's Changing Demographics

For companies looking to develop a Snapchat marketing strategy, it's important to realize that, like Facebook and Instagram before it, the app's demographics are beginning to skew older as Snapchat becomes integrated into mainstream culture and a broader range of users acclimate to Snapchat's functionality and features. Regardless of user age, however, Snapchat's astounding engagement rates (over 50% of Snapchat users are on the platform daily) and massive amounts of new content assures that Snapchat audiences will continue to spend hours on the app every week.

Snapchat Demographics For Marketers

1. The number of users aged 25+ is growing 2x faster than users under 25.

Snapchat added more 25- to 34-year-olds (an increase of 103%) and users over the age of 35 (an 84% increase) than 18- to 24-year-olds—the latter demographic only increased by 56%.

2. Over half of new Snapchat users are over the age of 25.

Snap Inc. revealed that its user base is quickly expanding into older demographics, with over 50% of U.S. daily new users being over 25. This represents a unique opportunity for advertisers to reach early adopting, older generations through social media.

3. Users 25+ access Snapchat 12 times a day, totaling 20 minutes.

The Snapchat community spends an average of 25 to 30 minutes collectively on Snapchat each day and will check the app 18 times a day. For users over 25, Snapchat is used 12 times a day taking up 20 minutes of the day.

4. Over 60% of U.S. smartphone users between the ages of 13 and 34 are Snapchatters.

Smartphones are facilitating changes in communication habits, and Snapchat has taken part in this, with over 60% of U.S. smartphone users between the ages of 13 and 34 using the app actively.

5. Over 60% of Americans between the ages of 13 and 38 are on Snapchat.

60% of Americans ages 13 to 38 are using Snapchat on at least a monthly basis. Broken down, and incredible 41% of teens 13 to 17 are using Snapchat.

6. 60 million North Americans use Snapchat.

Snapchat’s current audience in North America totals at 60 million. Forecasts preceding Snap Inc.’s IPO suggests that Snap’s audience by the end of 2017 in the US alone will reach 70.4 million.

7. 85% of daily U.S. users are between 13 and 34.

18 to 24-year-olds make up the largest segment of Snapchat monthly U.S. users at over 37%. For the remainder of U.S. monthly users, 13 to 17-year-olds make up 23%, 25-34-year-olds make up 26%, 35 to 54-year-olds make up 12%, and 55+-year-olds make up 2%.

8. More than 30% of teenagers say that Snapchat is the most important social media platform.

A survey of 6,500 U.S. teens found that 28% named Snapchat as their most important social network, growing from 13% two years ago. Snapchat’s importance to teens can be attributed to the dominance of messaging apps in later years.

9. Users 25 years of age and under visit Snapchat over 20 times per day.

Snapchat found that users younger than 25 used Snapchat more than 20 times and spent over 30 minutes on the app. Revisiting the app multiple times throughout the day indicates that Snapchat has many user engagement opportunities.

10. Nearly 60% of college users would purchase a brand if sent a coupon on Snapchat.

Snapchat can be immensely resourceful to brands for advertising purposes. In fact, if brands offered a coupon to users through Snapchat, 60% of college students would be likely to make a purchase.
