HOW TO BUY SNAPCHAT STOCK (when it has an IPO)

6/17/2017 Update: Snapchat stock has gone down about $4 in the month of June so far and closed at $17.54 on Friday. That price is close to its all-time low of $17.00 per share. The stock continues to be a polarizing one with investors who can’t agree on whether the company has a platform that can truly stand the test of time and make money.

Much of the talk continues to center on Facebook’s attempts to copy Snapchat on many different fronts and what that will mean for the longterm future of Snap. Facebook is rolling out features such as filters, animations, and stories which directly copy Snapchat and that has really been giving investors a reason to doubt the stock. Right now, all investors who bought the stock anytime during the first two days after the IPO at $24 or higher are now all underwater. The average volume of shares traded, very high the first couple of days, has seen a big drop as more and more negative SNAP stories hit the Internet. Most analysts are very bearish on the stock and see SNAP as being overvalued.    

For anyone interested in buying Snapchat stock, I recommend taking a look at its financials which the company made public for the first time in early February. It is important to understand that Snap itself says in the filing that its shares “carry a high degree of risk“. In all the numbers that were revealed, Snapchat says it has 158 million daily users which bring in revenue of over $400 million in revenue. Last year the company had net losses of a little over $500 million which will surprise a lot of investors and might make some weary of buying the stock. It is also interesting that in that filing, Snap said that it sees itself as a camera company and that means hardware will likely be a big part of its future.     


Snapchat’s chief executive Evan Spiegel has now successfully pulled off one of the biggest IPO’s this decade and investors can finally buy Snapchat stock. Trading under the symbol “SNAP”, any investor who has money in a broker account that allows them to buy stocks on the NYSE can now invest in this exciting company. 

You might have seen news stories about vast sums of dollars being invested in Snapchat (the latest was during May – June of 2016) and that money came from investment firms and other big companies (like Yahoo) but not everyday investors like you and me. Those big investors have now been financially rewarded with this big IPO that gripped the finance world on 3/2/2017. But now smaller individual stock investors are wondering whether they can profit from Snapchat as well and finally get a chance to buy the stock.  


You Can Now Buy Snapchat Stock 

To buy stock in Snap Inc. you need to open an account with one of the many U.S. online stock brokers. (I’m sorry but I am unfamiliar with investors from other countries and what you need to do to buy SNAP stock). After opening your account and putting money in it, you will be able to buy and sell the stock any time the market is open. 

TD Ameritrade is one of the most respected online brokers and the one where I have my portfolio for this website. It charges a reasonable $6.95 fee per trade and it came in 3rd in Barron’s 2017 online discount broker rankings of more than 15 different brokerages. I recommend TD Ameritrade for investors of all levels and there are zero account minimums so it is a great choice for anyone new to stock investing.

**It is important to follow through and put money in your account (fund it) after opening it. One of the most common mistakes beginners make is they never follow through and start trading. So, after opening your TD Ameritrade account which shouldn’t take more than 30 minutes, make sure you get it funded so that you can buy Snapchat stock when the company has its IPO. I would also recommend doing some research and buying your first stock soon after funding your account so that you can get started investing.  


Facebook Tried To Buy Snapchat For $3 Billion

In late 2013, it was reported that Facebook offered 3 billion dollars for Snapchat but the offer was turned down. At the time, most people thought it was crazy to turn down that kind of guaranteed money for what was nothing more than an App that had yet to make any profit. 

But in light of Snapchat’s now successful IPO, turning down that $3 billion was a brilliant move. As of the day after the IPO, Snapchat was valued at more than $28 billion and will make its cofounders many more billions than they would have made had they sold out earlier. SNAP has been the most highly publicized IPO since Alibaba and looks to pave the way for many more technology IPO in 2017.
