What Is Snapchat?

Snapchat is a popular mobile app that lets users share photos and short videos. Originally designed for quick photos — or snaps — with a limited viewing time, Snapchat’s features have become so popular that many users turn to the app as a photo creation tool for pictures they save permanently to their phones and share on other social media platforms, and recipients have the option of taking a screenshot of the photo.

Snapchat also lets users create a 24-hour story comprised of several snaps that followers can view an unlimited number of times before they expire.

Snapchat has experienced significant growth in recent months, with a 350 percent increase in daily video views between May 2015 and January 2016. There are approximately 500 million Snapchat stories shared each day.

If you want these photos to feature your own distinctive geographic presence, you can make a bold impression on any local audience with our custom designed geofilters.

What Are Geofilters?

Geofilters are one type of filter, or overlay, that you can add to your photos and videos on Snapchat. Filters are built into the program and added with the swipe of a finger. Some filters are exclusive to a particular event or holiday, while others are standard options showing details like the current weather or the speed you’re traveling. Geofilters are those you can only add when you’re using the app in a specific location.

Filters can send a powerful visual message, and they’re instantly personalized by the user whose photo accompanies them. Finding a special geofilter is an exciting perk for users who never know what they might have access to in a particular spot.

A well-designed filter can send a targeted message to users and viewers, whether you’re hyping a store opening, encouraging charity participation, or simply celebrating a private party.

How Are Geofilters Mapped?

Geofilters, by their very nature, are specific to a particular area. When you have a custom geofilter created, you must specify your targeted location. Snapchat users won’t see your filter as one of their options unless they’re within range of your specified destination.

Once we have the coordinates for your geofilter, we can establish a geofence. This identifies the boundaries that users must be within to access your filter. At this time, on-demand geofilters are only available for a limited time before they expire. You must specify the dates and times that you want your geofilter to appear.

How Are Geofilters Used?

Snapchat users must enable location services to use geofilters. However, Snapchat doesn’t store the user’s location. Users who aren’t seeing any geofilters can check their location services under Settings in the Manage tab. Here, you can turn filters on or off. Within the app, you add geofilters to pictures and videos by swiping right.

How to Add a Geofilter to a Snap

Geofilters give Snapchat users the opportunity to add a fun, location-specific overlay to snaps. With Location Services and Filters enabled, Snapchat geofilters show up in thousands of places all over the world, from small towns to big cities, wherever Snapchat users can be found.

Turn Location Services On

It’s super easy to add a geofilter to your Snaps. Before you do, you’ll first need to navigate to your Snapchat Settings and enable Filters and Location Services.

iOS

Navigate to Settings, then Privacy, and finally Location Services. Turn these on for Snapchat.

Android

Navigate to Settings, then location, and finally Turn on.

Turn Filters On

Once you have enabled both your location, go back to the Snapchat app and access your Settings by clicking the gear icon. Under Additional Services, click Manage. Simply toggle Filters to the on position. Press “OK” when prompted to let Snapchat user your location.

Swipe to Add Your Filter

To add a Geofilter to your Snap, take a photo or video, then swipe left or right on the Snap preview screen until you have located the filter around you that you would like to use. When you’ve found the one you like, simply complete your Snap and send it out into the world!
