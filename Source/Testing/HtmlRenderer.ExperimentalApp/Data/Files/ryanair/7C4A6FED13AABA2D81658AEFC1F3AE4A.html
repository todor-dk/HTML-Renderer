    Ryanair

    Flying with Ryanair

    Ryanair is an Irish, low cost airline. Its headquarters are located at the Dublin Airport in Fingal, Ireland. Ryanair is one of the largest low cost airlines in Europe along with easyJet. From its 50 bases and with a fleet of 300+ Boeing 737-800 aircrafts, it operates about 1,500 daily flights to over 175 destinations across nearly 30 countries. In 2012, the company carried 79 million passengers.

    Online check-in

    All passengers must check in online and print out their boarding pass before arriving at the airport. Online check-in is available from 15 days up to 4 hours before the scheduled flight departure time.

    Passengers that do not print their boarding pass at least 4 hours before flight departure must go to the Ryanair check-in counter at the airport at least 40 minutes before departure to collect their boarding pass. A boarding pass re-issue fee of Â£70 per ticket will be applied.

    Ryanair airport check-in desks open 2 hours before the scheduled flight departure time and close 40 minutes before departure. The boarding gate closes 20 minutes before departure; any passengers arriving after this time will not be allowed to board.

    Checked baggage allowance

    Ryanair does not have a free checked baggage allowance. All checked bags are subject to a fee. Bags will not be accepted if they weigh more than 32kg or have dimensions larger than 81 x 119 x 119cm. Each passenger can check up to 2 bags of either 15kg or 20kg each paying the fee during the online reservation process. Checking a bag at the airport is considerably higher than when done online. Prices to check luggage depend on if it is low or high seasons, and also depending on if the bag weighs 15kg or 20kg. Bags exceeding this weight allowance will be subject to extra fees (Â£20 per excess kilogram).

    Cabin luggage allowance

    All passengers, except infants, have a hand baggage allowance of 10kg which includes 1 free piece of cabin luggage and 1 personal item. The new Ryanair hand luggage allowance defines a personal item as one of the following: handbag, briefcase, laptop, shop purchase, camera, etc.
