      We flew Ryanair from Sevilla to Valencia (and back). It is true that you constantly feel like Ryanair is trying to screw you over- from the requirement that you print your own boarding pass (or else they charge you) to the rules about luggage weight and size (if your bag is too big, they charge you) to the reminders that if you are not an EU resident, you must stop at the visa check desk before proceeding through security (or else they charge you). When I finished reading through the 10+ emails Ryanair had sent me in advance of my flight, I felt an intense wave of anxiety. But, truth be told, both of our flights were painless- no one was really checking the size or weight of anyone's bag (admittedly, no one had anything insanely big), the flight staff was friendly (and I actually felt bad for them having to constantly sell things - food, drinks, lottery tickets, BioParc tickets), and the flight was on time. For a budget airlines, they are way better than the American alternative (looking at you, Spirit Airlines). So, in short, play by their crummy rules, and it's a cheap and convenient way to get around Europe.

      They changed the boarding gate last minute and delayed the flight. They don't give you answers and make you feel stupid. Also, every time you fly with them you have to check in online if you don't want to get charged at the airport. Even if you do so, you have to stop by their desk to get stamped to cross the security. Ridiculous. Not worth the savings on the flight ticket.

      Don't believe I've EVER awarded a single Star before. My cheery disposition can usually change any awkward moment into great service and an amazing event. Really: I enjoy people! But the three women at the Madrid airport were openly rude to my wife and me. I understand there are different cultural expectations. Working with people is part of the excitement of travel! And in someone else's culture, the customer may not always come first. That's fine. No problemo. But the three women at Ryanair, Madrid, give Methusela nightmares.

      Ryanair is The Spirit Airlines of Europe... Where everything costs arm and a leg -- i.e. be it to choose your seat, to have a glass of water, to carry on a bag that exceeds the 10 kg weight and size limits, and to have your boarding card printed at the ticket counter. I read a lot of horror stories about people bringing in bags that fit the carry on size limits at check-in but were forced to check in at the gate when strict gate agents deemed these passengers' bags as too big. With those horror stories, I felt compelled to purchase a bag online, just so I could get some piece of mind. This turned out not necessary as passengers with very large bags, expanded and busted to the max, were let on without any problems or extra surcharges. Huh? What gives? On my flight from Madrid to Tangier, I waited about 30 minutes to check in my bag. Lines moved very slowly as hundreds of people - old and young - queued up to check in bags, etc. I was afraid that I was going to miss my flight with such a long line, but eventually I made it up to the ticket counter, checked my bag, and then I was on my way to the second ticket counter, for a RyanAir ticket verification stamping (which only took like 1 second). From there, security lasted about 15 minutes and passport control another 20-25 minutes (lines can be very long!). All in all, the exit procedures took about 1 hour total... Which left about 10-15 minutes extra to weave through Terminal 1 to get to the gate. It just happened that my flight to Tangier was late, due to late inbound aircraft and possibly the Madrid Airport strike. All in all, the flight was about 45 minutes late... Not too bad I suppose. One of the things I noticed, which I stated before, was that RyanAir customers in Spain rarely check in their bags even if they exceed the max size and weight. Huh? Almost all the passengers on my flight carried on pretty big luggage, but the gate agent didn't do anything about it. Why weren't their bags weight or sized, as I read online? Perhaps the Spanish division of RyanAir is more lax and laissezfaire? Not sure. Maybe it was just on my particular flight, since there would possibly be language barriers with some of the non-English or non-Spanish speaking customers! Not sure. At any rate, what I learned is that you probably will be okay without checking in your bag within Spain/Morocco flights. On my flight, I experienced something pretty scary: A few of the customers were using their cell phones during take off and landing! Granted that cell phone communication probably won't interfere with airplane/airport control tower communications, but there still could possibly be some possible risk of disruption or interference! The in-flight crew didn't do a very good job ensuring that all their passengers mobile phones were turned off, as I had experienced and evidenced! At least on the Madrid to Tangier flight, they should have more announcements, warnings, and precautions messages in English, Spanish, French, and Arabic to ensure all customers get the same message and comply with in flight crew instructions. My overall flight experience made me feel both a bit unsafe and a bit gypped. Yes, I could have saved 30â‚¬ for not checking in my bag, but I do tend to follow the old adage "better safe than sorry". Hopefully more communication messages will be executed for future Ryanair flights to ensure security and safety. Until then, I probably will not travel with RyanAir for a while.
