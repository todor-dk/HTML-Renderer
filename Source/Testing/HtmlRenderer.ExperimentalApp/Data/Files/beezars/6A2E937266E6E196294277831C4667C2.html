THE BEEZERS

The year was 1970. It was the second year in the ‘new’ high school for the Sophomore class of Paragould High School.

Coach Doy Pannell was the legendary basketball coach, having won the state tournament in 1963. PHS was always a power in basketball. The previous year, the Bulldogs with no starter over 6'2" went to the semifinals of the state tournament at the ‘Cowbarn’ as Coach Pannell called it. Barton Coliseum was the real name.

Little did everyone know that 1970 was going to be different. Both on the A team and the B team, as they were called in those days. Coach Pannell called the B Team ‘The Beezers’, and this is their story.

FEBRUARY 10, 1970

There were about 10 seconds left.

Coach Clinton Gore called time out. The Beezers were down by one.

Worse, they had not won a game all year. As was the norm this night, at the end of the 3rd quarter, the Bulldog Bs were down 17 to the Westside Warriors. Westside took several of their players to the dressing room so they could get ready for the A game. All seemed lost, yet again.

Down 44–27, the Beezers went for broke and started pressing. Ricky Wineland stole a ball and made a layup. Terry Horton hit his patented left handed setter from around the top of the key. Doug Wigginton got in on the scoring. Chuck Dudley hit 3 shots for 6 points in the run.

Suddenly, the crowd that was arriving for the A game sensed something was brewing at the Bulldog gymnasium. The students, the cheerleaders began to have hope, something not achievable all year so far.

Fighting hard, the Beezers closed to within one.

Coach Gore called time out, looked at us and called the play.

I (Chuck Dudley) was to get the ball and take the shot. This was entrusted to a guy who had never played organized ball until the start of the season. A chance for glory. Scared? Probably, it was a long time ago.

Our 6'8" tuba player from the band, Jeff Hoffman was inserted into the game.

The referee handed the ball to our player, and it was worked to me.

I found an opening, turned and shot off the backboard, using the square as I was taught……the ball looked good, hit the square…….bounded on the rim…………………

MEET THE BEEZERS

If you look at this team, there was not a lot of talent or experience. The best players from a pretty good 9th grade Bullpup team were placed on the A Team. As Richard Brummett, a senior that year would admit, there wasn’t a lot of basketball talent on the A team either. They lost some games where the local sportswriter claimed there were cobwebs over the Paragould basket. I think Dick Clifford was his name.

Brad Paul and Howard Smith were pretty good, and Roger Easton was on both teams.

That left us.

Roger Easton……a good player on the Bullpups, saw some A team action

Rick Wineland….our man Otis. Fast as he could be. Left hander

Doug Wigginton…….another good junior high player.

Mike Crabtree…….played in junior high

Steve ‘Doc’ Jackson……first time to give it a try

Terry Horton……my family rival in the car business, a port sider.

Joe Farley……..left hander (seems to be a theme), a junior on this team, golfer……..

Mike ‘Spike’ Debons……wanted the exercise, better known as a golfer at Fox Hills CC…also a junior

Jeff ‘Tuba’ Hoffman……recruited from the band. 6'8", ya just can’t coach height.

Franklin Diggs….played cause his buddies did. Famed for shooting a halfcourt shot that hit the scoreboard

Richard Curtwright……..moved in from Michigan….real fast……

Steve Maxwell………another southpaw……team jokester

Larry ‘Cowboy’ Hull was on the team, but didn’t make the picture for some reason.

Me……Chuck Dudley……..first time to play basketball (not in the picture…..this was taken right after our massive loss to Blytheville where I had taken an elbow to the teeth, and was getting a tooth cast put on by Dr Crabtree)

Coach Gore……at the time, tough, mean, liked to run us………and run……….and run………and run.

These were good ole PHS’s representatives on the hardwood, B Team style.

The 1970 version of The Beezers.

THE SEASON

The first game was at Westside. We lost by 20 and Coach Gore told us that this was not what Paragould basketball was all about………little did we know.

We lost one game 44–8…..

Lost at Blytheville 73–18………on a night when the roads were so icy that no parents made the trip. The night I tried to bite a Chickasaw’s elbow……..

We lost every game, in every way imaginable.

So how do you react to this? Well, the A Team wasn’t much better. It was just a bad year for Paragould, one that comes along every now and then in sports.

What most of the guys interviewed remember was all the running we did. Coach Gore was into ‘fitness’.

After we lost to Blytheville, the next day’s practice consisted of running bleachers. Running bleachers until the fans were coming in for the junior high game that night. Only Wineland and Curtwright didn’t quit or throw up. Terry Horton remembers that the parents came out the next practice and the bleacher running was a thing of the past……………

MEMORIES

I was able to interview most of the team members 47 years later.

Jeff Hoffman passed away a few years ago of cancer. A gentle giant, with a heart of gold. And a mean tuba player. The late Ron ‘Argyle’ Jernigan and I went to his funeral. Jeff was such an important member of our class.

Mike Crabtree may be living in Fayetteville.

Steve Maxwell was unable to be found.

Mike Debons…was a junior….played to get in shape for golf.. remembers Joe Farley throwing half court passes, and Spike missing all of them. Coach Gore asked him what was the problem and Spike said that Joe was throwing them so hard they curved like a baseball curve ball! Said this team was the most fun he ever had in school. At Trumann, Spike fouled out before halftime…..Joe shot the ball 15–20 times a game and only made 2 or 3…..wouldn’t pass the ball so Spike threatened him…..once after 3 minutes Gore called a timeout and asked him why he was sucking air so bad……..Spike said he was having a heart attack!! He now lives in Dumas, Texas, in the Panhandle. Self employed with service stations and contracting and painting companies.

Terry Horton……remembers all the running and scoring 6 points in the Westside game……….one of the teams left handers……last year for Terry to play, went on to long career in wildlife management…….now Marketing Director for FTN Associates in Little Rock, a company concerned with maintaining water quality in Arkansas. He lives in North Little Rock.

Doug Wigginton…..Doug hated our losing. He was on the good junior high team. Said he was a better defender than ball handler. This was Doug’s last year playing basketball and he said he regretted not going farther because he loved playing. Running bleachers………when he didn’t get put on varsity he decided to hang it up. Doug has been in the car business ever since he graduated, and been quite successful working in St Louis. He still lives in Paragould.

Roger Easton…remembers he was tired of losing and it was embarrassing. Steve Maxwell was cutting up after we lost in the dressing room and he had to keep a towel over his head while Coach was chewing us out to keep from being seen laughing……..haven’t seen Roger in a while and just recently made contact. Seemed to remember we were legends in our own minds. He is in charge of the power plant operations at St Vincent’s hospital in Little Rock. He lives in Benton.

Joe Farley…Joe was a junior……that’s Dr Joe Farley to you!! Joe remembers that against full court presses, he would throw the ball to Spike and he would drop the ball! Word for the team was ‘pitiful’……..Never played again after this year. His brother, Bones Farley, was very much into our games. He was a 9th grader and remembers the Beezers were must see games! Joe could drive so Bones tagged along. They remember Coach asking Spike who he was guarding and Spike said the guy in the black hair…….we were playing an all black school back in the day……..Joe became a doctor, and recently retired as an ER doctor in Springfield, Missouri.

Rick Wineland………our man Otis…….Rick was fast, and was a key in the Westside game, stealing the ball several times……thought it was remarkable that at one time there were FOUR left handers on the court at the same time…Rick went on and lettered as a Senior on the A team……..and played at a tech school near Camden after graduation………running bleachers……along with Curtwright, wouldn’t quit…….Rick has owned his own business and worked with real estate in Paragould. He said we chose to deal with what we had, and it was fun in the end, just not at the time. Rick has been a great supporter of the PHS class of 1972.

Richard Curtwright…….Richard came to Paragould our 9th grade year…played football……..he was real fast. He and Jeff Hoffman became real friends as Jeff ‘convinced’ a couple of guys trying to give Richard crap as the new guy to bug off. Interesting story because Jeff was such a gentle man. Richard said we did the best we could do…….he went to Oak Grove as a junior and graduated from there. He has been involved with Christian rock music for many years and still is today. Been great to see him again after all these years.

Franklin Diggs………….as mentioned, Frank played because Doc and Terry did. Said he never scored during our year!! Frank has been a great supporter of the Class of 1972 over the years……..remember him having a big cooker and doing a big BBQ for the class. He and Otis get together quite a bit…Frank retired a few years ago and spends time with his grandkids and eating at the Iron Horse!!

Steve Jackson………..tried several times to get hold of Doc. He did text me once but never connected. He lives in Blytheville. Haven’t seen him since we graduated, I guess.

Larry Hull……our Cowboy. Coach Pannell called him Hully Gully because he was always in a hurry……remembers the running like all of us. Cowboy spent time in the military in Europe, learned to sing and pick a guitar……..worked in Branson at Shepard of the Hills, performing for many years. He now plays in the Bad Ass Bulldog Band with other class of 1972 members. Larry lives in Mt View.

Chuck Dudley……….didn’t play until sophomore year. Decided to play after being manager for the Bullpups. Spent all my time shooting………the Westside game was a big memory…….we almost beat Tech, and that would have been something…….made good friends with these guys back then. Earned 2 varsity letters, was able to start our Senior year, and was a walkon at the University of Tennessee at Martin………spent 40 years in the financial services business and lived in Hot Springs since 1993.

Coach Clinton Gore……….Coach is now 78…lives in Osceola after a long, successful coaching career….had 5 bypass surgery in the last couple of years. Paragould was his first job……..said he probably would do things a little different, but back then it was his way or the highway. He remembered the team tried hard………and putting Jeff into the game late. Little remembered…….he and Sammy Weir had a chance to go to the Big Shootout between Arkansas and Texas in December 1969………so David Meek coached us for two games………with no better results. I asked Clint what he would say to you guys after all these years, and he said: Tough times don’t define who you are, and that he is proud of all of you…….he was glad to hear from me (I’ve seen him over the years) and to hear about this team.

WHAT OTHERS REMEMBER

Greg Marlar had no idea of our record. Said he just remembers cheering for us, no matter, because it was us. Greg was always in the pep band, and we are grateful for his contributions.

Talked to all the cheerleaders with the exception of Vicky Messer.

Carol Higgins Elmore was a junior……..she loved cheerleading, and wanted to win, a true Paragould Bulldog. Whether we won or lost, she would always be for us. She remembers having to try out in front of the student body for cheerleader……something I didn’t remember.

Suzie Brummett Bean said she didn’t remember anything!! C’mon Sue!! But we loved having her there on the sidelines. Our Senior year Coach Doy let us sit with the cheerleaders on the bus coming home from road games, and Suzie and I sat together a few times…….

Debbie Jackson Maddox………also remembered having to try out in front of the student body………..and remembered the crocheted beanies we had. My Grandmother, Doty Dudley, did a lot of them. She lived across the street from my great grandmother on 6 1/2 street. Debbie was a great one and is still a big part of the PHS class of 1972.

Brenda Fletcher Garner……was glad to be a cheerleader……didn’t matter if we won or lost…..also remembered trying out in front of the student body. Loved it, was exciting…….had no clue how good/bad we were! Remembers everybody talking to everybody….a great bunch of people.

Richard Brummett……to the point, he said we were ‘terrible’!! It seemed our plan was to get the ball to a post player who couldn’t catch it…..Jeff did need some work there. But then again the A team didn’t have a great year either…….and so it was…..

I remember so much for some reason………..one day Jeff said he was 7 for 11 on rebounds…..I said what do you mean by that Jeff? I tried for 11 and got to 7!! Only Jeff could say that………Remember Joe Farley had this circle move, he’d go round and round and then shoot it. Hully Gully would drive into the lane with no fear and throw up a shot. We all hated running line drills……..and those dreaded bleachers.

HOW IT ENDED………..

The shot banked off the rim…….time stood still………..I had missed, we would lose again………and then……..there was Jeff Hoffman, who Coach Gore had put back in the game……

Some say Jeff put his big ole hands up and it bounced back in the basket…..some say he rebounded it and put it back up…the guys were uncertain about this…no matter…

IT WENT IN THE BASKET!!!… time had run out and the lowly Beezers had outscored Westside 18–0 in the 4th quarter to win. Bulldogs……The Beezers win 45–44…………

Fans, cheerleaders ran on the court……Bones Farley ran on the court. Maybe Steve Block ran on the court…..it was pandemonium, like we had won the national championship. For me, I realized it was the first winning game I had ever played in. Maybe the loudest noise in the gym that year. All the adversity seemed to just fade away. We had won! We actually won!!

A simple B team game in February 1970…one little remembered by most folks…...but, the story stands the test of time.

It was the only game we won, finishing 1–19. No matter, we got better, and moved on to other things.

WHAT ARE THE LESSONS FROM THIS SEASON

It’s said that tough times forge good character. If so, we all should have great character!

In the end, none of us were defined by that year and team. As a team, we all had to go through this together, and so there is that bond from long ago. Win/lose……..it doesn’t matter now. We learned how to cope, we learned to lose and get back up and fight again. We ran a lot. We laughed a lot. We learned to be for each other. We learned to care about each other. Isn’t that what sports is supposed to teach?

We went on to have families, careers, good times, bad times. Maybe the lessons we learned helped us along the way.

All I know is my memory of these guys, and this team, is all good. I love you all and was honored to sweat, run, play and wear the uniform of Paragould High School with you.

God bless and Godspeed Beezers!!

Chuck Dudley
