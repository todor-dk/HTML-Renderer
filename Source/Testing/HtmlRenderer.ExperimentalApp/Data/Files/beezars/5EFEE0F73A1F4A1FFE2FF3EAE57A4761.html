      Beezers. Reopened in a new location. Yay! No fries. Boo. No grill either. Just hot dogs, some wraps, and some paninis. More kinds of hot dogs to make up for this, but that doesn't make it better for me. Beezers was a Chicago Dog joint, through and through in it's old location. In the new location, it helps the craving a little, in that there's little other options, but it's no longer a place I would drive an hour to get to. That dog on those fries was a combination that made the angels sing, but the dog alone is just okay and the other options that you'd never see in a real Chicago dog joint make it feel forced, to me - like the "california tart" froyo flavor at the ice-creamesque froyo places. Also, the ice cream is gone. This is not a substantial loss, in that their new location is in the same building as the new Trombetta's (and ice cream joint, if you didn't know), so you can get ice cream after your dog, but but I can't really count it as part of THIS business anymore. Also, the soft serve is gone, which is a shame, and if you want to get a shake with your dog and burger (wait - no more burgers - no grill - either), well, you'll have to go wait in another line for that at the adjoining ice cream business. Honestly, I would give Beezers 3 stars now, but the people who work there are divine. The service is truly outstanding and worthy of that extra star. Overall, it's no longer a place I'd drive cross state to get to, but if I'm hungry when passing by, I'd gladly filled my belly with a Chicago dog or two, and continue to mourn the loss of the best fries I've ever had.
