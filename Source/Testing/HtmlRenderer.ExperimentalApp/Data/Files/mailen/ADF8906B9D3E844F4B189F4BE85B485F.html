Paul J. Mailen II, Sterling Twp., died Wednesday in the Fox Chase Hospital. His wife of 25 years is the former Linda McLain.

Born Dec. 25, 1952 in Scranton, he was the son of Paul J. Mailen, Scranton, and the late Ruth Ann Evans Mailen. Paul was a man of character, understanding, generosity and love. He was a 1970 graduate of West Scranton High School and a member of the 1970 Class A basketball Eastern final team. He was employed at RCA/Thomson Electronics for 30 years. Because of his love of basketball, he became a PIAA official and practiced it for 22 years. He mostly enjoyed folllowing his children's athletic careers; his boys were his world, and his wife was his absolute soulmate and the love of his life.

His perspective of life was no nonsense, simplicity and unconditional love, always putting others before himself. A friend to many, he will be sadly missed by the lives he touched and his big black Lab, Damien.

The family would like to extend its heartfelt thanks for the loving care by Dr. Armando Sallavanti, Dr. Vitaly Geyfman, Dr. Alvin Sharma and Fox Chase Cancer Center's Drs. Neeta Somaiah and Allan Haber.

Also surviving are two sons, Ryne Richard Mailen, Sterling; and Paul J. Mailen III and wife, Marygrace, Old Forge; two grandsons, Andrew and Connor Mailen; a twin brother, Craig J. Mailen, West Scranton; a brother, Scott A. Mailen and his wife, Karen, Annville; a sister, Jill Golden, Gouldsboro; a mother-in-law, Clara A. McLain, Sterling; and nieces and nephews.

He was also preceded in death by his father-in-law, Richard A. McLain Sr.; and a brother-in-law, Richard A. McLain.

A memorial service will be held Monday at 11 a.m. in the Sterling United Methodist Church, Route 196, Sterling. Friends may call Monday from 9 until the service at the church. Interment, Fairview Memorial Park, Elmhurst.

In lieu of flowers memorial contributions may be made to the Griffin Pond Animal Shelter, 967 Griffin Pond Road, South Abington Twp., PA 18411; or to Fox Chase Cancer Center, 333 Cottman Ave., Philadelphia, PA 19111.

Arrangements are by the James Wilson Funeral Home Route 296, Lake Ariel.

For directions to the church or to send the family an online condolence, visit www.james wilsonfuneralhome.com and click the link to Paul's obituary.
Published in Scranton Times on June 24, 2010
