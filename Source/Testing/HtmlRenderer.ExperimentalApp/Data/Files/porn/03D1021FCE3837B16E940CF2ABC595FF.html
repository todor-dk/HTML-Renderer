English[edit]

Etymology 1[edit]

From πορν- (porn-), the stem of the Ancient Greek πόρνη (pórnē, “prostitute”).

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Received Pronunciation) enPR: pôrn, IPA(key): /pɔːn/

Prefix[edit]

porn-

  1. (rare) prostitute
Derived terms[edit]
Derived terms
  • pornerastic
  • pornocracy
  • pornocrat
  • pornosophical

Etymology 2[edit]

From porn(ography) and porn(ographer), both ultimately also from πόρνη (pórnē, “prostitute”), as porn-¹.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Received Pronunciation) enPR: pôrn, IPA(key): /pɔːn/

Prefix[edit]

porn-

  1. Relating to or constituting pornography.

Usage notes[edit]
