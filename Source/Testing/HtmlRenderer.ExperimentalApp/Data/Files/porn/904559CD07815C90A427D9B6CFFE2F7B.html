  • Hanmers on Lundy Island in the Bristol Channel, UK 

    Hanmers was built by a fisherman in 1902. He chose a good site, a dip in the hill, on the path from the beach to a castle, so the place is sheltered but has the usual wonderful view out to sea towards Devon. It is weather-boarded outside and its interior is also of wood, painted white in the front rooms, which gives it a warm and solid feel. Hanmers is managed by The Landmark Trust, a charity that restores historic buildings.
