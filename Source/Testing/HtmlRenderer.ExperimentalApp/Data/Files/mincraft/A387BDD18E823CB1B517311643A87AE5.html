Anna Momibelle B.: Hi Steve,
I understand your frustration. We are, on so many levels, struggling to make Minecraft work for us in an EDU setting, and many of the powers that be might not understand completely the hurdles (tech, financial and otherwise) that we face.
I have used clients and server-side modded Minecraft well before MinecraftEDU was a thing. Yes, kids know that modded Minecraft play is the challenge they may seek. It won't be anytime soon that we will be able to 'convert' them to the classroom setting that is MinecraftEE.
That said, MinecraftEE is the perfect solution for teachers introducing Minecraft in the Grs 2-4 classroom. It offers the right kind of control and ease of access that teachers will need, albeit at a cost. When we present kids Grades 6-7 and above with the game, it ought to be with the prerogative to meet them on their playing field, so to speak. Working with them to encourage great social-emotional skills, fair-play, etc. This is where we can break from the structure, but this is where the commitment on the part of a teacher or parent, will be key. It's also, dare I say it, the domain of the after-school club set (like myself).
However, I do know of some teachers that have given the reins over to more mature students. They run games using mods such as Industrial Craft, to facilitate learning.
So much more to say here ... but just wanted to say ... I understand where you are coming from.
: )
Anna

Shane Asselstine: Just pointing out to run 3 servers, you would be required to purchase 3 server software licenses of MinecraftEdu.

Also, disagree with separating the player levels. I find that the best way for players to learn is to work with those that have played. Unless you think you are going to have time to teach them how to play during this event.

Instead have a creative, a survival, and a project server and just mix up the groups based on who wants to play in what style. For your project idea, make sure it is a project that could not be completed by a single student.

Go to the MinecraftEdu wiki and learn how to set up servers. Its relatively easy.
