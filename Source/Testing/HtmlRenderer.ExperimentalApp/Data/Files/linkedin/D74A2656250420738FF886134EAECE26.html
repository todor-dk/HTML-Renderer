How to Link Facebook and Twitter With LinkedIn

by Beth Bartlett

Social media is a valuable way for business owners to keep up with customers, peers and vendors. By connecting your accounts on Facebook, Twitter and LinkedIn, you have the opportunity to grow your professional network and show off your communication skills. Connecting these accounts can also deepen consumer loyalty if you offer special information or discounts to your customers and interact with them on a personal level.

Linking Facebook to LinkedIn

1. Sign in to Facebook. Along the left side of the news feed are different sections such as Favorites, Groups and Apps. Click "Apps" and "App Center." You can find the LinkedIn app faster by entering it in the search bar at the top. When the results come up, click "Apps" under Search Filters, and the official LinkedIn app will appear at the top.

2. Click on "Go to App," and it will take you to the first page of the LinkedIn application. Click on “Connect With Facebook,” and a window from LinkedIn will pop up asking for your LinkedIn email address and password. In this window, you can adjust your Facebook privacy settings before the application imports your LinkedIn information, allowing you to limit who sees your LinkedIn updates on Facebook. Once you've set your preferences, click “Log in With Facebook.” The application will import your information and ask you if you want to connect with any of your Facebook friends on LinkedIn. You can pick and choose or select “Skip This Step” if desired. There are four more steps inviting you to expand your LinkedIn network with email contacts, people you may know on LinkedIn and Facebook, and a page where you can enter contacts manually.

3. Click on one of the two LinkedIn buttons on the final step of the import process. The buttons offer you a choice of a "Free Basic Version of LinkedIn" or an upgrade to a paid account. After you make a selection, your Facebook account and LinkedIn account are connected. You can share your updates from LinkedIn on Facebook, and LinkedIn will occasionally post to your Facebook wall with opportunities or recent connections you've made.

Linking Twitter to LinkedIn

1. Log in to LinkedIn and click on your "Profile" page. Under your name, work experience and education, you’ll see a link to add a Twitter account to your profile.

2. Click on “Add a Twitter Account,” and a pop-up window will open from Twitter. Enter your username and password, and click to allow the application to access your Twitter account.

3. Close the window, and your Twitter account is now a clickable link on your LinkedIn profile page, allowing anyone viewing your page to click and view your tweets. You can also share LinkedIn updates with your Twitter followers.

Tips

  • Privacy is always a concern when sharing data between sites. Use different, complex passwords for each site.
  • Remember what you tweet on one site can be read by someone on LinkedIn, and your LinkedIn information may be read by someone on Facebook. If you're connecting all your media, be professional in your communications.
