About ESPN The Magazine:

ESPN The Magazine is a bi-weekly sports magazine published in the United States. Its writers cover Major League Baseball, the National Basketball Association, the National Football League and the National Hockey League, as well as college basketball and football. The magazine is known for its relatively lighthearted tone as it offers opinion pieces, articles about the business aspect of sports, and information about breakthrough athletes as well as veterans who are well-known to fans.

ESPN The Magazine is known mainly for the opinionated articles it publishes. A column written by Stuart Scott called Two Way answers questions posed by readers who are dedicated sports fans. The magazines writers offer predictions about teams and competitions and go into detail about their predictions. One article gives an opinion about what the landscape of the National Football League will look like three years from now.

Readers of ESPN are treated to articles about the business side of sports. One article titled Brian Cashmans Winning Ways contained in the Money issue of the magazine talks about how the general manager of the New York Yankees has been able to build such a strong franchise. Another talks about the ways in which two-time Superbowl winner Troy Polamalu of the Pittsburgh Steelers manages his money.

ESPN The Magazine offers articles about breakthrough athletes as well as veterans known to many in the United States. An article titled What Makes a Hall of Famer? makes cases both for and against the induction of Eli Manning, quarterback of the New York Giants, into the NFL Hall of Fame. One entry appearing in the magazines Next issue offers a detailed list of the twenty players that the magazines writers feel define the future of sports in America.
