Best Answer:  It was the American University IN London (a different institution from the American University OF London) which was fined by Islington Council in London for misleading students about its university status in 2006.

http://www.islington.gov.uk/Council/CouncilNews/PressOffice/2006/01/2380.asp

Have you got the right place?

It doesn't really matter, both places are unaccredited and of similar repute in the academic world I'm afraid. You write that you're "not concerned" whether the university is accredited or not, but you ask "What will the degree I get be like? would it say that I studied in an institute?..." These issues are VERY closely interlinked. Unaccredited learning institutions aren't regarded as universities in many parts of the world, and a 'degree' from an unaccredited institution isn't regarded as a real degree either. This is certainly the case in the UK. So, if you are worried whether your qualification will be a real degree from a real UK university, it certainly won't be from either institution I'm afraid. Accreditation is everything here.

I would think very carefully about whether you should spend several more years of your life studying for an unrecognised non-degree and think about studying for a real degree at a real university. I have known many overseas students who have done this in London. I would definitely get some professional advice if you feel that you have been misled in some way too.

ps I checked UCAS just in case and I couldn't find either of them on there.

pps [29 Nov] "In the UK it is illegal to offer a qualification that is or might seem to be UK degree unless the awarding body is recognised by the Secretary of State, a Royal Charter or Act of Parliament to grant degrees. Prosecutions under the Education Reform Act are rare, as many unaccredited awarding bodies are based outside UK jurisdiction. It is also worth noting in this context that the Business Names Act 1985 made it an offence for any business in the UK to use the word "university" in its name without the formal approval of the Privy Council."
Source(s): http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/School_accreditation
