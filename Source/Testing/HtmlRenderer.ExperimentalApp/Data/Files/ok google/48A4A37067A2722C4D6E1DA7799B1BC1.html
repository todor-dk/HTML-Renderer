Google Now Update To Bring ‘Ok, Google’ Offline And More

By Arjit Singh -
July 3, 2015
0
Share on Facebook
Tweet on Twitter
  • tweet
GARD Pro Not Registered

A huge breakthrough was recently announced by Google. Google Now, the famous Android-based voice assistant, will soon become available offline, and several new features are also on the way.

GARD Pro Not Registered

Offline Google Now will probably have some limited functionality, but it’s still a massive update and we can’t wait to see it in action. All these changes are coming in the 4.8 update.

Something that we’ve been expecting from voice assistants for a while now has been basic phone-control. No voice assistant manages this at full potential right now, but Google is planning to change this completely. Using ‘Ok Google’ will allow you to control volume, screen luminosity, network control, as well as sending texts, playing music and improved calling capabilities. In addition, Google Now will send you notifications about unsecured networks and you’ll also be able to shut down your Chromecast simply through voice recognition. This is a massive deal but it’s just the tip of the iceberg for Google. Many of these new features were discovered by the community itself in the latest Google Now APK which was recently leaked to the tech community, and a lot of people were surprised.

Google Now to Offer offline Functionality

For a little while Google has added some impressive location-based mechanisms to its personal voice assistant. With location awareness, your phone could potentially fetch data based on location alone. In any case, the big breakthrough here is that the personal assistant will work offline, and it’s the first one to do so successfully and actually offer its owner some visible control. This will definitely put Google Now way ahead of its competition, which has been very harsh lately with the addition of Hound, Siri and Cortana. None of these voice assistants are so full of features, and 4.8 will fill Google Now with even more features than before, and we can’t wait to see the whole functionality in action.
