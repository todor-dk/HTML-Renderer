How do I use "Ok Google" offline?

"OK Google" is the voice command used to activate Google Now voice search on your Android smartphone, as well as other Google devices such as its Google Glass smartglasses.

You can only use OK Google to perform certain voice-search functions without an Internet connection. Here is the some of its function.

The list of suggested offline commands included Play Music, Open Gmail, Turn on Wi-Fi, Turn up the volume, Turn on the flashlight, Turn on airplane mode, Turn on Bluetooth, and Dim the screen. There are some other commands you can use with having working Internet connection.

UPDATE - For voice typing do the following.

Need to visit voice settings. Found under language and input. Or through Google Now settings>voice.
Bonus in Google Now settings, can enable "OK Google detection"

Download whichever language you wish to use offline when speaking.

IMPORTANT - Download the Offline speech languages and change your device language to English(US).

How do you change the OK Google command?

To do that, first install Tasker and AutoVoice (if you haven't already) and do the following:

  1. Open your phone's Settings app.
  2. Tap "Accessibility."
  3. Tap "AutoVoice Google Now Integration."
  4. Tap the toggle at the top of the screen.
  5. In the dialog that pops up, tap "OK."
