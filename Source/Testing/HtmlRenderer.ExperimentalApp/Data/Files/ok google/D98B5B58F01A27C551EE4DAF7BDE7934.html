Replace OK Google Search Widget on Samsung Galaxy S5

By Jack Armijo | Galaxy S5

7

You’ve grown to love the OK Google search widget on your Samsung Galaxy S5.  But somehow you’ve lost it and you’re not sure how.

Here’s how you put the OK Google Search Widget back on a homescreen panel of your Samsung Galaxy S5.

Here’s my video tutorial on Youtube for How To Replace The Ok Google Search Widget on the Galaxy S5

Add A Widget

Long-Press on any open spot of the homescreen of your Samsung Galaxy S5.  Tap on the widgets icon.

Scroll through the list of widgets.  Tap on Google Search. (Note: In Android 5.0 Google Search has been renamed to Google App)

Long-Press on Google Search icon and drag it to the position on the homescreen.

Can’t Find Google Search in Widgets

So what happens if you can’t find the Google Search tool in the Widgets drawer?  You might need to redownload it from Google Play.

First open up Google Play by tapping on its icon.

 

Tap in the Search bar.

 

Type in Google Search. Tap on Google Search in the search results.

Note: Even if the Play Store says its already installed.  Go ahead and tap on install again.  It will open the existing app if its really already installed on your device.

 

Tap on Google.

Note: On the app description page in the Play Store; if it says Open instead of install then Google App is already installed and you need to go back to the top of this post and redo the first 1/2 of this lesson.

 

Next tap on Install to begin the download process and install the App.  Once the install is complete go back to the top of this lesson and do the first 1/2 to add the widget to your homescreen.

And that’s how you replace the OK Google Search Widget on your Samsung Galaxy S5.
