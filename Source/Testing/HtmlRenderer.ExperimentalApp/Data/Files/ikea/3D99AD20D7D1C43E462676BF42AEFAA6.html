        Oh IKEA, my survival furniture through college and now grown into completing my adult home.

        Be aware that IKEA does cater to all price ranges. From a $5 stool, to your $200 stool, you'll find it here. Best of all, the price reflects the quality. Though, you'd be surprised how long my $10 side table and $25 bookshelf has lasted me and that thing is made with recycled wood.

        Of course, coming to IKEA means taking a stroll through the showroom as well as the marketplace. Coming here with a mission is very difficult. You will somehow find the NEED to purchase many other items for your house. Suddenly you need 10 more items than you initially intended. Did they read your mind? Or maybe tapped into your deepest interior designer desires.

        Not feeling like having your kids follow you as you try out every single couch in the store? They have Småland, a play area that you can near the entrance. Not sure about details, but the website states that children 37-54" inches tall and potty trained can stay there. If you have the Family card, you will also get a deal, I think a free 30-minutes or something like that.

        Speaking of Family card, don't miss it. You also get a free coffee sometimes, and discount on your meal from the IKEA cafe. The cafeteria offers the famous Swedish meatball dish, now expanded to also include chicken meatballs and veggie meatballs. There's also the smoked salmon salad or sandwich wrap if you're not in the mood for meatballs. Downstairs, you can find the food market area where you can grab a $1 cone of yoghurt ice cream.

        Don't miss the as-is section, where you can find discontinued, display, and returned items for a fraction of the original price. We've found several furnitures and other home goods there for really cheap with only minor damages. Other times we're lucky and found brand new items that seems to be discontinued. Check it often, you'll be surprised what you can find!

        Customer service and return area has always been very helpful. We've recently returned the dresser that has been recalled and the process can not be smoother. Literally in and out in no time.

        Missing parts to your furniture? You can get many of the parts for free from the customer service.

        Tip: Planning to buy a lot of small items? Plan to bring a shopping bag because IKEA doesn't provide one for you. They sell the large blue shopping bags (made of tarp) that you can buy for $0.79. We got one and use it every time we go to IKEA. The bag goes right into the cart they've created specifically to hold the bags (those are the cart you see without the baskets).

        All in all... Who doesn't love IKEA? :)

        Ikea is one of my happy places, and this Ikea does not disappoint. It's located at the gigantic Potomac Mills mall and parking is very ample. Keep in mind you'll probably need a few hours to wander around, sit on all the sofas, admire all the countertops, and imagine how you'll add linens that ooze simple Swedish charm into your already cluttered home.

        I've been here several times before but my most recent visit shortly before Christmas cemented that it's the time to stop in and feel surrounded by holiday whimsy. But rather than the overwhelming marketing you get at other stores, this is a relatively subtle push to get you to buy winter decor.

        Obviously you need to make a pit stop at the food area for lunch. I'd recommend their classic meatballs if you're a meat eater. An Ikea family card gets you a free coffee or tea. And the cafe is conveniently located midway in your journey, at the end of the top floor leading to the downstairs level.

        As far as the products? After all these years, still hit or miss. I have two pieces of furniture at home from the collections made of actual wood, and although they were a pain to assemble, they look really nice and are study. Some of their plates and other cookware have also held up. But in contrast, I've gotten items here that last for a season rather than forever. I'd say be judicious in your pickings...and let yourself indulge a bit in the cutesy extra merchandise.
