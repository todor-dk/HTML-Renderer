First, Ikea and Portillo's. Here's what else Indy wants.

Here are the top 5 franchises Indy residents say they would like to see in the Circle City, but don’t hold your breath.

The jumbo chili dog from Portillo's offers the best of both worlds with a Chicago-style hot dog covered in hearty chili.(Photo: Provided by Portillo's)

1029 CONNECTTWEETLINKEDINEMAILMORE

Just 17 months later, two of your wishes already have come true: Ikea, the Swedish furniture retailer, broke ground on a new store in Fishers in October. On Wednesday, news broke that Oak Brook, IL-based sandwich chain Portillo's is coming to Fishers. Now we just need In-N-Out, Whataburger and Shake Shack to move in. In this story, published Aug. 7, 2015, IndyStar readers told us which franchises they wanted to see set up shop in the Indianapolis area.

Our wish list now has two vacancies. How would you fill them?

The following story was originally published Aug. 7, 2015.

Transplants to Indy have experienced it at least once. You’re craving something from home. You type the name into a search field (or look it up in the phone book, if you’re really old school) and that dream-destroyer of a phrase pops up: No results in your area.

Your heart sinks. How will you possibly cope without it?

And how is it even possible that Indianapolis doesn’t have that?

What “that” is depends largely on what part of the U.S. you come from. Or maybe you’re a lifelong Hoosier who has a hankering for that place your cousin Calvin in California keeps raving about?

Whatever it might be, we were curious to find out. So we asked. We invited you through our IndyStar Facebook page to let us know what particular franchise you’d most like to see come to town.

Out of 1,199 responses, we narrowed it down to a top five — hint, you’re really into Swedish furniture and burger joints — and then tried our best to find out just when, if ever, your wish might come true. And, no, this isn’t going to end well for some of you. OK, maybe any of you.

But here goes:


Ikea

(688 votes)

 (Photo: Provided by International Design Museum.)

What it is: Swedish furniture store, famous for affordable furniture that comes with indecipherable instructions (not that we’re still bitter about that crib we assembled). Also meatballs.

The closest one is in: Suburban Cincinnati or suburban Chicago. OK, Chicago we get, but Cincinnati? That hurts.

Why we want them: “Oh my goodness, I love Ikea, put me down for Ikea!” said Jaquanda Eskew, an Indy resident originally from Gary, Ind.

When are they coming to Indy? Who knows? We called the Swedish retail giant but no one responded for comment. So we asked Mark Perlstein of Indy-based Sitehawk Retail Real Estate, who thinks Indianapolis is definitely on their radar: “I think Ikea's looking at all major metropolitan areas. They’re thinking about their proximity to other stores in Cincinnati, Detroit, and Chicago and how does that cannibalize on the market.”


In-N-Out

(240 votes)

 (Photo: AP photo / Adam Lau)

What it is: A California-based fast food favorite selling burgers, fries, and shakes. (And, yes, cousin Calvin, we know this is blasphemous but think Five Guys without the peanuts.)

The closest one is in: The Dallas/Fort Worth area. Anyone feel like taking a quick 13-hour drive?

Why we want them: “I love that everything is so fresh and made to order,” said Sarah Conrad, an Indy resident who was born and raised in California, who loves the secret menu. (Pssst. Want that animal style?) “It’s an overall good atmosphere that can appeal to all ages! It would be a great place to have in Indy!”

When are they coming to Indy? Sorry, but In-N-Out reps told us the company practice of keeping food fresh means they can only expand as far as a one-day drive from their distribution facilities. If they do decide to come, it would be a long, slow process.


Portillo’s

(148 votes)

 (Photo: John Samora / The Arizona Republic)

What it is: Chicago-based chain selling Italian beef, Chicago dogs and cake shakes.

The closest one is in: Merrillville, Ind. (Hey, it could be worse).

Why we want them: Michael Oliver, a Chicago native and student at Indiana University-Purdue University Indianapolis, said he craves Portillo’s like no other. “Their ribs are to die for and they have a huge variety of food,” he said. “It would definitely fill a niche in the city.”

When are they coming to Indy? Brace yourself for some pain. “While we have plans to take Portillo’s to new markets, we do not currently have any plans to come to Indianapolis,” wrote a Portillo’s representative in an email. But he said there was “no reason in particular” why they wouldn’t come. Want to feel even worse? Portillo’s has locations in, get this, Buena Park and Moreno Valley in California and Tempe, Ariz. Still, maybe there’s hope. “I do know that they recently were purchased by another company and they’ve announced that they are expanding in Illinois and Florida,” said Mary Chapman, senior director of product innovation at Technomic, a research and consulting firm focused on the food industry. “They’re picking their markets very carefully. I don’t know why they wouldn’t come to Indianapolis because it is so close.” We don’t either.


Whataburger

(75 votes)

 (Photo: Provided by whataburger.com.)

What it is: Texas-based burger joint, also known as “water-burger.” (Don’t tell anyone, but the burgers aren’t even the best thing on their menu. That honor goes to the addictive honey butter chicken biscuits.)

The closest one is in: Looks like it’s about seven hours away in Trussville, Ala. That said, if you find yourself just about anywhere in the South or Southwest, there’s likely going to be a Whataburger near by.

Why we want them: “Oh my god, I love Whataburger!” said Stephanie Rendon, an Indy native. “We went to Gulf Shores, Ala., a few years ago and we hit Whataburger and it was fantastic.”

When are they coming to Indy? Uncertain, but it doesn’t look good. Whataburger did not respond to our query. But Chapman, the food industry analyst, wasn’t optimistic. “There’s a lot of competition for hamburger chains, so it makes sense to grow closer to home. Jumping over to Indianapolis would be a pretty big leap. According to our database 80 percent of their stores are in Texas, and there are plenty of markets they could fill in before they go that far north.”


Shake Shack

(48 votes)

 (Photo: AP)

What it is: Trendy New York burger joint, known for burgers and shakes. (Not surprisingly, it draws comparisons to, you guessed it, Five Guys and In-N-Out.)

The closest one is in: Chicago. (They’re like that big brother that always gets the good stuff first).

Why we want them: “If there was a shake shack in Indy I would die,” tweeted @HinshawKate, accompanied by a heart-eye emoji.

When are they coming to Indy? More bad news. Kinda. A rep for Shake Shack said there are no plans right now to expand to Indy, but — but — maybe they’d come here someday. “They have a couple units in Georgia, a couple in Texas, a couple in Illinois; they’re not sticking close to home,” said Chapman. “The way they’re hopping around, they may be looking at the major metros first, so maybe Indianapolis is on the plan B list.” Hey, that’s better than Plan C.

So, there you have it. Not the best news, but then again, we can’t really complain that much. And who needs all these chains, anyway. We can always eat our feelings over an order of shrimp cocktail at St. Elmo’s or a pork tenderloin sandwich at Edward’s Drive In, washed down with a Mug n Bun homemade root beer.

Take that Chicago.
