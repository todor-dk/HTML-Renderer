    Terrific Two Wheelers The 12 Best Buys in Motorcycles for 2017

    IKEA Made a Bike

    And it's not built of particle board.

    By Andrew Moseman
    Apr 13, 2016

    Could you find yourself bringing home a bicycle from the same place where you buy minimalist chairs and stock up on Swedish meatballs? This is Sladda, the unisex bike by IKEA that's set to go on sale in Europe this summer.

    Sladda is IKEA's attempt to build a bike for everyone, at least everyone 12 years old and up. The handlebars are adjustable, the aluminum frame lightweight. The wheels come in 26- or 28-inch varieties. The bike is meant to be as low-maintenance as possible, with the gears hidden out of sight and a belt taking the place of the bike chain. You can add on accessories like the basket and trailer seen here with a click, and IKEA hopes its bike becomes a platform for which third parties built new pieces.

    Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

    The cycle will cost 699 euros (about $800) for the general public. No word yet whether the Swedish giant plans to bring its Bikea to the U.S., nor how many sizes of allen wrench you'll need to put it together. I, for one, look forward to trying to assemble a bike with only graphic instructions.
