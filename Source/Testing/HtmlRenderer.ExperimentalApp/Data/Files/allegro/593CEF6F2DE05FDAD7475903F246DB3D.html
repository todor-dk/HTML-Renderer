The Allegro Story

The Allegro School was founded in 1989 as a non-profit 501 (C)(3) corporation to provide quality education, keep autistic children with their families, and prepare them for community living. The school serves approximately 60 students ages 3-21 from 10 counties in Northern and Central New Jersey. Approximately 100+ children and adults are served though our respite, and adult services departments.

Learn More

Our Mission

The Mission of Allegro School & Programs is to provide quality services to educate and support persons who have Autism / PDD, as needed, throughout their lifespan ever mindful of quality of life, the intent to maximize each individuals potential through a highly specialized, intensive and individualized education from which one can transition to less restrictive and inclusive settings, when appropriate in order to enable our adults to function productively in the community.
​

Who We Are

A highly effective individualized ABA Program for students ages 3 - 21 and adults on the Autism Spectrum. All of our programs align with the NJSLS while providing a well rounded education with access to individualized instruction, fine arts/music, daily living/life skills as well as Community Based Instruction. Our educational program is supported by our comprehensive OT and Speech Therapy Departments.

How We Achieve

Allegro School’s curriculum provides a structured and pragmatic view of learning. We connect academic subject matter to meaningful experiences across a variety of settings and the individuals embedded within them. It promotes intellectual development, personal growth and active citizenship.  As such, it involves other individuals (community members, workers) as well as instructors in the learning process.
