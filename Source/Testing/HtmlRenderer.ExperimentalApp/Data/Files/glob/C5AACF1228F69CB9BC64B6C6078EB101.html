Esperanto[edit]

Etymology[edit]

This entry lacks etymological information. If you are familiar with the origin of this term, please add it to the page per etymology instructions. You can also discuss it at the Etymology scriptorium.

Noun[edit]

globo (accusative singular globon, plural globoj, accusative plural globojn)

  1. globe

Derived terms[edit]

Terms derived from globo
  • duonglobo (“hemisphere”)
  • globkrajono (“ballpoint pen”)

Ido[edit]

Etymology[edit]

From Esperanto globo, from English globe, French globe, German Globus, Italian globo, Portuguese globo, Spanish globo, Russian гло́бус (glóbus), ultimately from Latin globus.

Noun[edit]

globo (plural globi)

  1. globe, orb

Derived terms[edit]

Terms derived from globo
  • globeto (“small ball”)

Italian[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin globus.

Noun[edit]

globo m (plural globi)

  1. globe

Derived terms[edit]

Terms derived from globo
  • globo oculare (“eyeball”)
  • globo terrestre (“globe of the world”)

Latin[edit]

Noun[edit]

globō

  1. dative singular of globus
  2. ablative singular of globus

References[edit]

  • globo in Charlton T. Lewis and Charles Short (1879) A Latin Dictionary, Oxford: Clarendon Press
  • “globo” in Félix Gaffiot’s Dictionnaire Illustré Latin-Français, Hachette (1934)

Portuguese[edit]

 globo on Portuguese Wikipedia

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin globus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • (Portugal) IPA(key): /ˈɡlo.βu/
  • (Brazil) IPA(key): /ˈɡlo.bu/
  • Hyphenation: glo‧bo

Noun[edit]

globo m (plural globos)

  1. globe
  2. sphere, ball

Spanish[edit]

Etymology[edit]

Borrowed from Latin globus.

Pronunciation[edit]

  • IPA(key): /ˈɡlo̞βo̞/

Noun[edit]

globo m (plural globos)

  1. balloon
  2. globe
  3. (tennis) lob

Synonyms[edit]

  • (balloon): vejiga (El Salvador), chimbomba (Nicaragua)

Related terms[edit]

  • glóbulo
  • global
  • (diminutive): globito
