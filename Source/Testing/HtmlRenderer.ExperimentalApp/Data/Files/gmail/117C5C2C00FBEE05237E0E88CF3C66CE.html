Gmail Login Help - Recovery Tips

User Rating: 4 / 5

 

Whether you are looking to recover a Gmail account you haven't used in a while, or you have just found yourself locked out of the account you use every day, recovering your password and is a process that should not take long.

When you get yourself locked out of your account, there may be a couple of causes for this. These include trying to enter a wrong password because you cannot remember the correct one, being told that your account does not exist, and forgetting the username for the account. The following guide will help you recover your Gmail account so you can use it again without problems.

Recover an Account for Which You Don't Know the Password

One of the most common causes for getting locked out of your Gmail account is forgetting your password. With many people using a browser that is configured to enter the password of email accounts automatically, it often happens to don’t know what to enter in the password filed when the information is lost. Follow the steps below to recover access to your Gmail account when you forgot your password. Remember that this only works when you have genuinely lost the password, not in the case your account was hacked.

1.    Go to the Gmail sign-up page and enter your username. Click on the "Need help?" button located below the "Sign In" button.   
2.    On the Screen that appears, click on "I don't know my password".
 
3.    Enter your Gmail account address when requested.
4.    Enter the last password you remember when prompted.
5.    Depending on the recovery options you opted for when you set up the original account, various choices may appear. These include getting a verification code on your phone, confirming access to a recovery email address associated with your Gmail account, or answering a security question. Click on the option you want to use.
6.    In some cases, you may be prompted to solve a Captcha verification before continuing.
7.    If you opt for a phone verification code, a screen that says "Google just sent a verification code via text message (SMS) to your phone number" will appear. Enter the code in the blank field provided.
8.    Create a new password and re-enter it in the field on the next page.
9.    If you opt for recovering your password via your recovery email, you will get an email with a link you need to click to verify the ownership of the account. Click on it and follow the on-screen instructions to create a new password.
10.    If you opt to recover the password by answering a security question, answer it when prompted. This method only works for old Gmail accounts.


Recover Your Gmail Account with the Account Recovery Form

In some cases, none of the methods mentioned above works, so you may need to fill out the password recovery form. This often happens when you do not have access to a phone, or when you do not have any phone number or alternative email address for recovery purposes associated with your Gmail account. If this is the case, you will see a window that says "Account help for (your username).
 
Follow these steps to fill out the Account Recovery Form correctly. Keep in mind that by using this recovery method you do not get instant access to your account, as a Google employee has to verify the information you provide.

1.    Enter an alternative email address where Google can contact you. If possible, you should be the owner of the address.
2.    On the next page, choose the last time you were able to access your Gmail account. This does not have to be an exact date if you don't know it, but try to be as accurate as possible.
3.    Enter the date when you created the account. You can use an approximation again if you are not sure.
4.    Answer the security question when prompted. This step will only be available for those who have a security question associated with their account.
5.    Enter up to five email addresses that you send or receive emails from frequently.
6.    Choose some of the other Google products you use, together with the approximate dates you started to use them.
7.    Click the "Submit" button to complete the review. Google will review your form and let you know the results on the email address you provided.


Recover a Gmail Account When You Forgot Your Username

If you forgot your username, you need to provide Google with some personal details before being able to access your account again. Follow the steps below to retrieve your username.

1.    Visit Google's username recovery page at https://www.google.com/accounts/recovery/forgotusername.
 

2.    Choose from two options to recover your username. Depending on what type of security options you chose when you created the Gmail account, opt to recover the username by entering your recovery email address or your recovery phone number.
3.    Next, enter our first and last name.
4.    Check the "I'm not a robot" button and type the letters in the Captcha picture.
5.    If you opted for email recovery, an email will be sent to your recovery address with the usernames associated with that email address.
6.    If you chose the phone option, you will receive a verification code on the phone. Enter it in the blank field when prompted, and click on the "Continue" button. You will be able to see a list of usernames associated with that particular phone number.

Recovering Tips for Hacked Accounts

If your Gmail account was hacked, it is often possible to regain access to it via the recovery methods described above. However, if the hacker managed to delete the account, you can only try the Account Recovery Form, but in some cases you will not be able to retrieve it. Unfortunately, Google does not re-use account names, so if your account is deleted, you cannot create one with the same username. If a hacker deleted your contacts, you can recover them as Google allows users to restore deleted contacts in the last 30 days.
