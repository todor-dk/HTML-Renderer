About Saint Louis
Satellite view showing St. Louis, an independent city in east-central Missouri United States, located on the west bank of the Mississippi River opposite East St. Louis.

St. Louis first became incorporated as a town in 1809, today the city has a population of about 354,000 inhabitants, 2.8 million people live in the Greater St. Louis metropolitan area.

Some Attractions in St. Louis
The Gateway Arch, a 630-foot (192 m) tall monument to President Thomas Jefferson. The Missouri Botanical Garden and the Citygarden. The Butterfly House, a three-story crystal palace is filled with up to 2,000 butterflies in free flight. You might also explore the Museum of Transportation, the City Museum and the Missouri History Museum. Cahokia, the site of an ancient Native American city is directly across the Mississippi River.
