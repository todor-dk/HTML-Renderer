Binary options trading is in a nutshell a kind of trading where a fixed profit is earned by simply predicting correctly if the price of a particular asset will go up or down over a certain (short) time period (often as little as one minute) – if the prediction is that the value will go up, this is referred to as a “call” option; on the other hand, predicting that the price will fall is known as a “put” option.

The underlying assets can take a number of forms, such as:

- Commodities, such as gold, silver, oil, corn, wheat, coffee etc
- Forex, in other words the values of currencies for example US dollars, EUR, GBP, AUD, JPY and so on
- Market Indices like Nasdaq, FTSE, Dow Jones, Nikkei and so on
- Shares in listed organisations such as Google, Coca Cola, HSBC etc
