Best's Market Segment Outlook

An Outlook for a specific market segment within a geographic region indicates the potential future direction of the aggregate company ratings within that market segment over the next six to 18 months. Outlooks typically are reviewed annually; however, they can be revisited at any time during the year, depending on financial and market conditions.

A market segment outlook reflects a variety of factors, and A.M. Best's assessment of how these factors will affect company ratings within a particular market segment over the next six to 18 months. The key items considered include, but are not limited to: aggregate financial trends within the segment; current and forecasted economic conditions; the current regulatory environment and any pending changes; emerging product developments; and competitive issues.

In addition to being displayed below, market segment outlooks also can appear in A.M. Best press releases, Best's Special Reports, and more.

Like a company's Best's Credit Rating Outlook, a Best's Market Segment Outlook can be positive, negative or stable.

  • A positive outlook indicates that A.M. Best expects to take more positive rating actions (i.e., rating upgrades and/or outlook revisions) than negative rating actions (i.e., rating downgrades and/or outlook revisions) on companies operating within a market segment. Note: A positive outlook on a specific market segment does not mean that the outlook of all companies operating within the market segment will be positive.
  • A negative outlook indicates that A.M. Best expects to take more negative rating actions than positive rating actions on companies operating within a market segment. Note: A negative outlook on a specific market segment does not mean that the outlook of all companies operating within the market segment will be negative.
  • A stable outlook indicates that A.M. Best expects that rating actions within the market segment will not show a general trend and will be driven primarily by individual company operations.
