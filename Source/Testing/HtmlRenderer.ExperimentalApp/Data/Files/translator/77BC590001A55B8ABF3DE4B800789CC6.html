Description

Microsoft Translator is a free, personal translation app for 60+ languages, to translate text, voice, conversations, camera photos and screenshots. You can even download languages for offline translation for free to use when you travel!

• Text translation into over 60 languages*, for online and offline use
• Camera translation to translate text within photos and screenshots
• Voice translation to translate speech, and a split-screen mode for two participants having a bilingual conversation
• Multi-person conversation translation - connect your devices and have in-person conversations with up to 100 people across multiple languages
• Phrasebooks for verified translations and pronunciation guides to help you learn important phrases in foreign languages when you travel
• Look up alternate translations and meanings of a word to find the best translation to express yourself
• Download languages for offline use for when you travel without an internet connection
• Hear your translated phrase out loud to help you learn how to say the translation
• View transliterations (pronunciation guides) to help you pronounce the translation (includes Pinyin support for Chinese)
• Share your translations with other apps
• Pin and save your most frequent translations for later
• Translate websites on Safari using the Microsoft Translator browser extension
• Speak to your Apple watch to get an instant translation
• Automatically sync translations between your phone and Apple watch


Translator supports the following languages:  Afrikaans, Arabic, Bangla, Bosnian (Latin), Bulgarian, Cantonese (Traditional), Catalan, Chinese (Simplified), Chinese (Traditional), Croatian, Czech, Danish, Dutch, English, Estonian, Fijian, Filipino, Finnish, French, German, Greek, Haitian Creole, Hebrew, Hindi, Hmong Daw, Hungarian, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Kiswahili, Korean, Latvian, Lithuanian, Malagasy, Malay, Maltese, Norwegian, Persian, Polish, Portuguese, Quer'etaro Otomi, Romanian, Russian, Serbian (Cyrillic), Serbian (Latin), Slovak, Slovenian, Spanish, Swedish, Tahitian, Thai, Turkish, Ukrainian, Urdu, Vietnamese, Welsh, and Yucatec Maya.

Microsoft Translator is powered by cutting-edge technology used by Office, Bing, Skype, Internet Explorer as well as by partners such as Twitter, Yelp, eBay, WeChat and more. 

Location Permission: Microsoft only uses your location to show you where conversation occurred. We display this location in your conversation history.

*Some features are not available in all languages.

Customer Reviews

Great App and few improvement suggestions
     
by Dreg2000

I am expatriate in China, this app is life saving... I use it every day.
A few improvement easy suggestions:

1) Please do not delete the written text when swapping input<->output language!
It happens all the time writing a sentence to translate and realize that input<->output languages are swapped. Press on the button to put in the right way and need to re-type everything... (because the swap deleted the just written text)

2) Would be nice if the keyboard language would be set based on the input language. When input language is Chinese, then KB language should be set also in Chinese. Today this has to be done manually and therefore swapping languages needs 2 actions (swap the language and swap the KB). I think this could be done automatically

3) I take screenshots all the time (web pages, TaoBao online shopping, etc...) the translate the screen shots using the app.
This needs frequent access to the last taken picture, and is currently a bit painful process.
It would be nice if selecting a picture translation, the app would propose by default the last taken picture)

Anyway, even with little usability imperfections, it remains a great app.

Maybe the developers are reading the reviews and who knows, maybe it will become an even greater app.

Thank you Microsoft for providing such an app for free!

Best translator so far
     
by Laurel Light 1

I really like the feature to take a picture and it then translates...great when having to translate documents or web pages. It doesn't translate it perfectly - which is why I'm only giving it 4 stars - but well enough to understand the gist of it. I find it to be more accurate than both iTranslate and Google Translate.

In German they can have a string of words put together as one word, which sometimes doesn't translate. (Tip: try separating into the individual words.)

Another issue I have is when in typing mode. If I change from portrait to landscape (usually by accident, due to auto rotation) it wipes out all I've typed. So frustrating!

Pretty good translator
     
by Nicki garage

Microsoft has really stepped their game up with their recent upgrades to their translation services. The Apple Watch app is probably my favorite addition since it's pretty convenient and allows me to translate phrases without pulling out my phone. The quality of the translations has also improved, but not quite on par with Google Translate just yet; but it's getting there. The conversation feature is also pretty handy. I can hold a conversation with a non-English speaker (though the languages supported are limited for the time being) without having to wait my turn to speak like Google Translate app.
