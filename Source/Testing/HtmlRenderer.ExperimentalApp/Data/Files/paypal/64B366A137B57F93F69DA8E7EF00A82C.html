What is PayPal?

PayPal is an online payment system that is used for making online and mobile payments to persons, web shops and/or other pay sites. To be able to make payments using this method, you will need an email address and a conventional bank account or credit card that can be connected to your account.

Founded in 1998, PayPal was one of the pioneers in making online payments before it was subsequently taken over by eBay in 2002. The company obtained an official European banking license in 2007 and continues to grow at a rapid pace, with more than a hundred million users from an ever-growing amount of countries and currencies.

The best feature of PayPal is that their online payment service is completely free of charge, as the end-users (consumers) don’t need to pay for money transfers. The fees for payments are instead charged to the sellers rather than the buyers.

If you want to make payments on the internet by PayPal, you need to open a “funding” account using your normal bank account or credit card. However, because the payments are not carried out directly from your bank account or credit card, sensitive (financial) information will never be visible for the party that receives the payment. Therefore, PayPal is a great online payment option for people who are afraid that their identity may be stolen or personal data misused.

PayPal also allows you to receive payments from other persons, online casinos, and other pay sites. The money that you receive in your account can be used to make other online payments, or you can cash out an amount to the credit card/bank account that it is connected to.

Despite its success, PayPal is still rarely found at the cashiers of online casinos. However, there are some NetEnt Casinos recommended by us that do accept this online banking method for making deposits.
