How Do PayPal Claims Work?

by G.D. Palmer

  • 1 How to File a PayPal Appeal
  • 2 How to Get PayPal Seller Details

  • 4 How Does PayPal Handle Fraud?

PayPal makes buying and selling items online simple and convenient. However, the other user doesn't always follow through on her promise to deliver. If you pay for an item via PayPal but never receive it, you can create a dispute. Should the seller fail to communicate with you during the dispute, you have the option to escalate it to a claim.

Time Frame

PayPal requires you to file all claims within 45 days of the transaction. If you wait longer, PayPal will deny the claim automatically. You must also make sure you used PayPal, and not a credit or debit card, to pay for the items in question. You can escalate any dispute to a claim within 20 days of opening the dispute but should wait at least 7 days from the date of payment to file a claim for items not received. You cannot reopen or escalate closed disputes.

Escalation

To escalate your dispute to a claim, log in to PayPal and click “Resolution Center.” Choose “View” next to the dispute you would like to escalate, then click “Escalate this dispute to a PayPal claim.” Follow the on-screen instructions to tell PayPal about your problem, then click “Escalate to a Claim.”

Process

Once you've created a claim, PayPal may contact you for more information. Respond promptly, and provide clear-cut information about your case. If you don't respond when asked, PayPal will cancel the claim. If the seller doesn't respond, PayPal will automatically find in favor of the buyer. The company makes a decision on your claim within 30 days. You can see the claim status in the Resolution Center.

Seller Appeals

Even if PayPal resolves the claim in the buyer's favor, sellers can appeal. If a buyer uses or damages an item and then sends it back to you, returns an empty box or returns the wrong item, PayPal may reverse the claim as fraudulent. Sellers can file appeals by selecting “Closed Cases” in the PayPal Resolution Center, then choosing “Appeal.” If a seller appeals the claim, he must provide documentation, file a police report or fill out an official affidavit.

Considerations

If you paid for your item through a PayPal checkout but using a credit or debit card, you still have options. Contact your credit or debit card issuer and ask them for a chargeback. This reverses the transaction. According to PayPal, buyers most often request a chargeback when the item didn't arrive or when it was significantly different than the seller's description. You can also request a chargeback if someone used your card to buy an item through PayPal without your permission.
