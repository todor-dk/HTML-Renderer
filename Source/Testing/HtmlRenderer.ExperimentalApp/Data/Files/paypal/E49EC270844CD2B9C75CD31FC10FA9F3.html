Frequently Asked Questions

We know that financial terms can sometimes be confusing, so we've created some frequently asked questions to help explain things.

Of course, if you still have any questions, please contact us.

Q. How do I get a PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. There is one way to get a PayPal Prepaid Card.

  1. Visit a retail location to purchase a temporary PayPal Prepaid Card. There's no credit check and you will be able to load money to the card when you buy it, but the card will need to be activated and your identity verified in order to: a) receive a personalized card; b) add more money to the card account; and c) access more of the card's features, such as direct deposit. If you do not activate the temporary card within 120 days of its purchase, it will expire and become unusable. See below for more information about where the card can be purchased and what it means for you if your temporary card is not activated and it expires.

Q. How much does it cost to get a PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. There is a cost to purchase the temporary card at a retail location and the price depends on the retail location. You will also need to add funds at the time you purchase the card. The card purchase price depends on the retail location. Depending on which retail location you visit, the minimum initial load value will be either $10 or $20. The maximum initial load value will be $500.

Upon purchasing the card, you will be automatically enrolled in the $4.95 per month fee plan which lets you make unlimited purchase transactions (subject to funds availability). The first Plan Fee will be deducted from your Card Account within 36 hours of purchase. To view this monthly fee, and the other costs associated with using the card, see the "Fees Related to the Service" section of the PayPal Prepaid Card Cardholder Agreement.

Q. Who can purchase a PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. Any U.S. resident or U.S. resident alien over 18 years of age with a valid Social Security number (SSN) and a verifiable physical U.S. street address may purchase a card. We do not offer PayPal Prepaid Cards to residents of Puerto Rico, Guam, the U.S. Virgin Islands, American Samoa, or other U.S. territories. When you open a Card Account, you will be required to supply your name, physical street address (no P.O. Boxes), date of birth, and SSN.

Q. May a minor request or purchase a PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. No. A minor may not independently request or purchase a PayPal Prepaid Card. However, the primary cardholder of a PayPal Prepaid Card account may request a secondary card, at no additional cost, for a minor. The primary cardholder must be the parent or legal guardian of the minor. The minor must be at least 13 years of age. To request up to two secondary cards for eligible minor(s) or other household members or trusted friends, contact PayPal Prepaid Card Customer Service at (866) 753-6440.

When the cardholder requests the addition of the secondary cardholder(s) to the PayPal Prepaid Card account, the Customer Service representative will need to know each secondary cardholder's name, physical street address (no P.O. Boxes), date of birth, and SSN. In addition, a copy of each minor's Social Security card and birth certificate will be required.

Q. If I am not a U.S. citizen, may I request or purchase a PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. Yes. As long as you are a permanent resident of the U.S., over 18 years of age with a valid Social Security number (SSN), and a verifiable physical U.S. street address (no P.O. Boxes) you may request or purchase a card.

Q. How long does it take to receive a permanent PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. When you purchase the card at a retail location you will leave the store with a temporary card, which expires 120 days from the date of purchase. In order to receive a personalized card, you must activate the temporary card and verify your identity. You should follow the instructions that come with the temporary card. We will mail the personalized card to you on the first business day after you requested it. If you do not receive your personalized card within 10 business days, call Customer Service at (866) 753-6440.

Q. Why do I need to activate the PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. Successful activation of the temporary card you purchased at a store and verification of your identity means you will be able to enjoy more features like direct deposit, and withdrawing cash at Automated Teller Machines (ATMs).

Q. How do I activate my PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. To activate a PayPal Prepaid Card call our 24-hour toll-free automated phone system at (866) 753-6440, or visit www.PayPal.com/prepaid.

When you purchase the temporary card at a retail location and make an initial value load of up to $500, you will have the use of a temporary card with limited functionality: the temporary card cannot be reloaded with additional funds and it will expire 120 days from the date of purchase. In order to have access to more features of the card account, including receipt of a personalized card, access to the Virtual Account feature, and the ability to make additional value loads, you must activate the card and verify your identity following the instructions shown above or those that accompany the temporary card.

Q. What information do I have to provide when I activate my PayPal Prepaid MasterCard®?

A. There are a few key items needed to successfully activate the card. We must collect your first name and last name, a verifiable physical U.S. street address (no P.O. Boxes), date of birth, home phone number, e-mail address, and Social Security number.

A. Federal law (The USA PATRIOT Act) requires us to obtain, verify, and record certain information including your first name and last name, verifiable U.S. street address (no P.O. Boxes), date of birth, Social Security number, and other information that will allow us to reasonably identify you. We may also ask for your driver's license or other identifying documents at any time. These requirements help prevent the card from being used for illegal purposes.

Q. Is the money on my PayPal Prepaid MasterCard® safe and secure?

A. Yes. The PayPal Prepaid Card account is a safe and secure payment alternative. If your card is lost or stolen, we will, upon your request, send you a new card. Q. Will I receive a new PayPal Prepaid MasterCard® when my personalized card expires?

A. If our records show a balance on your PayPal Prepaid Card approximately 45 days prior to its expiration date (the date printed on the card) we will send you a replacement card with a new expiration date. Your funds will be fully accessible through use of the replacement card. Please be sure to regularly review your contact information so that we can deliver the replacement card to your correct address.

If our records show your PayPal Prepaid Card has a zero balance approximately 45 days prior to its expiration date (the date printed on the card), we will not send you a replacement card with a new expiration date.

An expired card cannot be used to make purchases, withdraw cash, or be reloaded.

Do not keep it in your wallet, or write it on your PayPal Prepaid Card. Never share your PIN with anyone (except that you may share your PIN with a secondary cardholder with the intent of allowing the secondary cardholder to access your card account). If you share your PIN with a secondary cardholder, you are wholly responsible for that person's use of the Card Account according to the terms of the PayPal Card Cardholder Agreement.

When selecting a PIN, remember to choose a 4-digit number that you are familiar with so that you don't easily forget it — but don't choose a PIN that's so obvious that a fraudster could easily determine what it is.

Q. Why is a hold sometimes placed on my money?

A. Gas stations, hotels, and car rental agencies frequently preauthorize a transaction, which means the business places a hold on your card until it knows the final amount you owe. Here are some common situations where such a hold will occur:

  1. Using a PayPal Prepaid Card to pay for gasoline at the pump: Let's say you fill your gas tank with $35.00 worth of gas. The gas station will place a hold on your card account for up to $100 or more. Three days later, the gas station will tell us (that is, settle the transaction) the actual dollar value of the amount you spent. That means the additional funds (in this example, $65 or more) that were subject to the hold will be released back to the card, thus making those funds available to you.

    You can avoid the hold by taking your card to the gas station cashier and indicate either a specific amount of fuel, or the specific dollar value you wish to spend
  2. Using a PayPal Prepaid Card to pay for a car rental: The PayPal Prepaid Card may be used at car rental agencies that accept prepaid cards. However, the rental agency will usually require a deposit of up to 20% or more than the cost of renting the vehicle. The rental agency will place a hold on your card account for that amount of money. The hold could last for a couple of weeks, and also depends on how long the rental lasts. We suggest you call the rental agency to inquire about how much they require for a standard deposit when paying with a prepaid card and how long a typical hold for a prepaid card will last.
  3. Using a PayPal Prepaid Card to pay for a hotel room: The PayPal Prepaid Card may be used at select hotels that accept prepaid cards. However, you should expect the hotel to place a hold on your card. The hold will cover the hotel room rate plus up to 20% or more of the cost of the room. The hold will be in place until the hotel settles the transaction, i.e., sometime after you check out of the hotel. A typical hold could be released the day you check out, or up to several weeks after check out occurs. We suggest you call the hotel to inquire about how much a typical hold will be if paying with a prepaid card, and how long a typical hold on a prepaid card will last.
