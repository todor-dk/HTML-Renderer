Map Your Neighborhood

As more and more information about the risk of a major earthquake/tsunami event due to Cascadia Subduction activity has been presented to the public, it has become clear that residents need to take steps to prepare for such an emergency.  Recognizing the fact that in the event of a disaster, your most immediate source of help is the neighbors living around you, approximately 100 Dungeness Heights residents attended Map Your Neighborhood (MYN) emergency preparedness sessions sponsored by Clallam County Emergency Management during the summer of 2015.

The purpose of MYN is to teach neighbors:
  • The 9 steps to take immediately following a disaster.
  • How to develop an inventory of neighborhood skills and equipment.
  • How to map their neighborhood and identify areas of concern, such as propane tanks.
  • How to support neighbors who are injured or need extra help -- such as elderly or disabled people, children and pets.

Dungeness Heights MYN Areas
Because Dungeness Heights is such a large neighborhood, a map was created and smaller MYN groups were formed based on geographical location.  Areas (numbered below) include residents living on:
  • Lower Brigadoon Blvd., Balmoral, Christian Lane and Karie Ct, Moonlight Dr.
  • Lighthouse View Dr., McCarter Pl., Knight Glen and central Brigadoon Blvd.
  • Brittany Lane.
  • Upper Brigadoon Blvd.
  • Moonlight Dr. (Participating residents are assigned to area 1)
  • Eberle Lane.
MYN Area Teams
All of the geographical areas described above use the same MYN Area Team structure.  Each Area Team comprises a Captain/Co-Captain and four Team Leaders assigned to:
  • Communications Team (primarily use walkie-talkies)
  • Propane Team (shuts off propane tanks as needed)
  • Triage Team (Basic First Aid and status reporting)
  • Care Team (ongoing care and TLC for injured or disabled people, children and pets)

MYN Area Teams hold periodic drills to practice walkie-talkie use and emergency response procedures.
