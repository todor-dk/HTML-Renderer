    Working at Amazon

    Employees receive comprehensive benefits starting on day one that include health insurance, 401(k) matching, bonuses and company stock awards.

    We pay our fulfillment center employees 30% more than traditional retail stores. Over the course of the year, on average, nearly 90 percent of employees in our fulfillment centers are regular, full-time employees. Seasonal employees help us to manage the variations we see in customer demand, such as during the busy holiday shopping period

    Innovative Benefits

    Amazon’s Career Choice Program pre-pays 95% of employees’ tuition for courses related to in-demand fields, regardless of whether the skills are relevant to a career at Amazon. Since the program’s launch just four years ago, more than 7,000 employees in 10 countries have pursued degrees through the program.

    Employees in our fulfillment centers also receive the same benefits as corporate employees, including robust paid maternity and parental leave options.

    Environment & Sustainability

    At Amazon, we are putting our scale and our inventive culture to work on sustainability and protecting our environment and many of Amazon’s fulfillment centers have sustainable and eco-friendly features. For example, four of Amazon’s fulfillment centers in the U.S. received LEED certification; these facilities were constructed with recycled building materials and feature natural lighting, resource-efficient fixtures and lighting controls.

    We’re also experimenting with wind, solar and fuel cell installations. Just recently we announced a new 253 Megawatt Wind Farm in West Texas that will generate 1,000,000 megawatt hours of wind energy annually – enough energy to power almost 90,000 U.S. homes.

    Giving

    Amazon gives back locally. We recently provided grants to more than 350 community organizations and since 2012 we have donated more than 6,000 Kindle e-readers and Fire tablets to enhance reading and learning opportunities in schools across the U.S.

    Military Hiring

    Amazon actively recruits U.S. veterans. In 2015, Amazon hired more than 4,400 military veterans into full-time roles across the company and we’ve pledged to hire an additional 25,000 veterans and military spouses over the next five years.
