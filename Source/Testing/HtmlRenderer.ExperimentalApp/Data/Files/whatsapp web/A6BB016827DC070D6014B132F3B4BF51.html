WhatsApp on PC: What is WhatsApp Web and How to Set it Up

By Doug Lynch -
July 13, 2015
1
Share on Facebook
Tweet on Twitter
  • tweet
GARD Pro Not Registered

Toward the end of January we saw the WhatsApp developers announce a brand new interface for the massively popular messaging service, WhatsApp Web. Let’s take a look at what this new feature is and how you can set it up on your computer right now.

GARD Pro Not Registered

Restrictions

WhatsApp has been notorious for the amount of restrictions that has been implemented into the service. You have always had to have a mobile phone number with the service and it has only been available for download on a smartphone. This means that anyone who doesn’t have a phone number that can receive SMS messages, and anyone who wants to use the service with a tablet has been left in the dark. While those restrictions are still in place, the company has started to be a little more lenient when it comes to how we can interact and communicate with our friends.

Announcement

Back in January of this year we saw the official announcement about WhatsApp Web when one of the founders went to his Facebook page to make the announcement. This wasn’t a huge change and you still had to have an account set up on your phone, it was nice to see that we could message our friends on the web. WhatsApp founder tells us that the web client acts as an extension of your phone and that the website is just mirroring what you see on the phone. None of the messages are stored on their servers or your computer and they continue to reside on your phone.

Setup

Once you have your WhatsApp account set up on your phone, all you have to do is visit the web address http://web.whatsapp.com/ in Chrome and you’ll see a QR code appear. Next, you will want to launch the WhatsApp application and while you are on the main chat list tab(not in a conversation), tap on the 3-dot menu button at the top right and select the WhatsApp Web option. You’ll then be instructed on how to scan the QR code on your screen and after you do this, you will be ‘paired’ with the PC. You’ll then see your messages and contacts in Chrome and from here you can use the messaging service just like you would on your phone.

GARD Pro Not Registered

You’ll actually be able to manage all of your logged in PCs directly through the WhatsApp mobile application. This is an interesting feature because it means that you can connect your WhatsApp account to multiple computers at the same time. This is also great because it means that you can use WhatsApp on a friend or public PC(like in a Library) without having to stay glued to your phone during conversations. Then when you’re ready to leave, you can simply log out from that PC on your phone and that will be it. Since none of the messages are stored on the PC, you don’t have to worry about any personal data being left behind.

Mobile Data

Another interesting use of WhatsApp Web is that using the service can save your precious data. Since the WhatsApp messages and images/videos are going through the computer’s internet access, you won’t use up any of your mobile data by using the service. This might not be a big deal for those with cheaper mobile service but mobile data can be very, very expensive in various places around the world. WhatsApp Web can not only make things more convenient but it can also save you some money as well.

Do we have any readers who use WhatsApp as their daily messaging client? I’ve been using the service for about 3 years or so and would like to hear what other people think about the service. We are also curious to hear what you think of the WhatsApp Web client as well so drop down into the comments section below to tell us your thoughts.
