WhatsApp QR Code: How to start using WhatsApp Web

Gautam Garg  |  March 5, 2015

We are all on Whatsapp. Okay maybe not all but at least 900 million of us. The popular mobile app is now allowing users to access their chats on the computer through a service called WhatsApp Web. And guess what? It is using QR Codes to verify your identity. No Email ID. No username. No password. Your smartphone is your key to use WhatsApp Web.

But first things first.

What’s a QR Code?

A QR Code is a 2D barcode that can store much more information than a traditional 1D barcode. The information can be decoded simply by scanning the QR Code with a smartphone application.

QR Codes are now commonly used for marketing & advertising, authentication, and inventory management. To know more about QR Codes, learn how to work with them.

How is WhatsApp Web using QR Codes for authentication?

When you first created a WhatsApp account, you were only required to provide your phone number and verify it. Now that WhatsApp is allowing users to access chats via their computer browsers, it needs to identify and authenticate them.

This is traditionally done via username and password but WhatsApp never generated such information. So the app is now using a neat way. Your WhatsApp app (already verified) on your smartphone is the key. Here is what you need to do:

1. Go to web.whatsapp.com on your computer browser. Important: WhatsApp Web currently supports only Google Chrome, Mozilla Firefox, or Opera browsers.

2. On the main screen, you will see a QR Code. This QR Code is dynamic in nature and will change every few seconds.

 

3. Open WhatsApp on your smartphone. Important: WhatsApp Web is available for iPhones, Android, Windows Phone 8.0 and 8.1, Nokia S60, Nokia S40 Single SIM EVO, BlackBerry and BB10 smartphones.

4. Click the ‘More Options’ button on the top right corner and click on ‘WhatsApp Web’

 

5. From this screen click on the ‘+’ sign on the top right corner to open the scanner.

 

6. Using this Scanner, scan the WhatsApp QR Code on your browser screen to complete the authentication process.

 

Now you will be able to view your chats on your computer as well. Note that you will need a stable internet connection on both your phone and computer for WhatsApp Web to work.

You can log out from WhatsApp Web easily from your WhatsApp on your phone.

That’s it! You are all set to use WhatsApp Web. Still have questions? Ask in the comments section below.

      Hi Khaled,

      The most important thing you need to know is that all your chat data is all stored only on your phone and not on any other server accessible via web.

      Whatsapp Web is simply a terminal to use Whatsapp via your phone. Think of it as a second screen. When you scan the QR Code, you authenticate your account. Then Whatsapp Web pulls your chat data from your phone in real-time. It also sends any message that you add on Whatsapp Web directly to your phone first and then to the recipient.

      This is why you need your phone to be on as well as with an active internet connection. Your phone is your server and to see your data on Whatsapp Web, you need your server to be active.

      Hope this clarifies your question.

      Thanks,
      Gautam

          This can be explained in two ways:

          1. Whatsapp is a ‘Mobile-first’ application: Whatsapp started as a mobile application and chose a model where all the data will be stored on the users device. Now when whatsapp decides to add another device (Whatsapp Web), technically the current method (using mobile as a server) is the only way to go about it.

          From a regulatory point of view, it might be possible that Whatsapp is not allowed to copy the users data from their devices to its web servers without first taking their permission.

          For Facebook, which started as a web app, having a web server was a necessity. For Facebook, it was much easier to scale to a mobile app using the same servers.

          2. Why Mobile Servers are better than Web Servers: I think this is debatable and largely depends on the strategy of the company. For companies that prefer to store data on Mobile (such as Whatsapp), the advantages are privacy of the users and possibly low cost of operations (no cost of maintaining web servers).
