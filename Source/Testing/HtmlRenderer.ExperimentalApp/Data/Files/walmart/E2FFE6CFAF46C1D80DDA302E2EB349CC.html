    • Who we are

    Each week, we serve nearly 260 million customers who visit our 11,504 stores under 65 banners in 28 countries and ecommerce websites and apps in 11 countries. With revenue of $486 billion in 2015, @WalmartLabs employs more than 3,500 associates worldwide.

    What we do

    We help people around the world save money and live better – anytime and anywhere – in retail stores, online, and through their mobile devices. @WalmartLabs has three development centers worldwide: USA, Brazil and India.

    @WalmartLabs is accelerating their development to redefine the shopping experience to meet the changing needs of our customers wherever they are —in a store, on our website, or on their mobile device.

    Team

    • Jeremy King

      CTO and Head of @WalmartLabs

      Jeremy leads @WalmartLabs, responsible for product, engineering, and webops teams who develop Walmart’s online business on a global scale. Jeremy has been on the forefront of cloud computing and brings over 20 years of experience. Jeremy’s focus has been on highly available and scalable web technologies, engineering methodology and productivity, and developing SaaS technology platforms and presentation layer frameworks to support global e-commerce operations.

    • Jaya Kolhatkar

      VP, Global Data

      Jaya is responsible for all data and analytical infrastructure, including the EDW, Big Data environment, real-time predictive analytics platform and BI tools. Jaya and her team are also responsible for managing transactional fraud for both online and offline orders. Jaya joined @WalmartLabs in June 2013 as part of the acquisition of Inkiru, a predictive intelligence platform company, where she was Co-founder and Chief Analytics Officer. Prior to co-founding Inkiru, Jaya worked at PayPal, eBay, Amazon, Bank One and Providian Insurance in the Predictive Analytics space.

    • Tim Kimmet

      VP, Cloud Platform

      Tim leads the Cloud Computing Platform at Walmart, code-named Strati. This includes common layers such as Infrastructure-as-a-Service, Platform-as-a-Service and Platform frameworks and services that support rapid application development at Walmart. The scope of this Platform is to dramatically increase the digital to physical integration of Walmart’s assets including @WalmartLabs Global E-commerce, Stores, Clubs, Distribution Centers and Fulfillment Centers. Tim is responsible for driving many of Walmart’s open source initiatives, including OneOps— a tool that manages continuous lifecycle management for business critical applications for all of Walmart’s properties (digital and physical). Prior to Walmart, Tim built similar platforms at scale for internet companies in Silicon Valley, including Ebay, PayPal and other startups prior.

    • Fiona Tan

      VP, Markets Engineering, Technology Strategy and Operations

      Fiona’s role is at the intersection of engineering, product innovation and technology strategy that powers @WalmartLabs. Her team delivers the product roadmap and engineering capabilities for our international businesses in the UK and Canada as well as our Sam’s Club business in the US. In addition her team drives the technology strategy and operational excellence across @WalmartLabs. She brings to @WalmartLabs extensive global experience, having built and operated 2 offshore centers of excellence and managed large global teams.

    • Shouvick Mukherjee

      VP, Growth

      Shouvick’s team builds world-class products that attract and engage shoppers and partners in new and innovative ways, integrating the experience for customers across stores, mobile and online. This role sits at the intersection of product, marketing and big data analytics and Shouvick has a proven track record across all areas. He brings close to 20 years of internet product and technology innovation experience. Before Walmart, he spent several years at Yahoo! Inc., serving most recently as Vice President Engineering, Consumer Platforms. He’s also held leadership positions in Advertising, Product & Engineering and led the R&D efforts for Yahoo! India.

    • LAURENT DESEGUR

      VP, Front End Engineering and Mobile

      Laurent leads front-end and mobile engineering in the development of a seamless customer experience across Walmart’s desktop and mobile sites and apps. Laurent’s team builds experiences that bridge the digital and in-store experience across all devices for Walmart’s customers. Laurent has proven experience in architecting and shipping large scale distributed systems from the ground up. Before Walmart, he served as Senior Director of Mobile Consumer Engineering at PayPal, where he was responsible for global consumer products on mobile devices. In a previous role, he led the design and deployment of Zynga’s mobile presence and oversaw the delivery of its products on smartphones.

    • Vijay Raghavendra

      VP, Category Development

      Vijay and his team of engineers, data scientists and product leaders are responsible for the analytics, tools and platforms to support our merchandizing strategies including competitive intelligence, algorithmic pricing, product catalog and marketplace. In addition, Vijay leads the technology teams responsible for product search. He came to @WalmartLabs by way of the acquisition of Inkiru, a predictive intelligence platform company, where he was a co-founder and the CTO. Prior to Inkiru, Vijay held leadership positions in a number of companies such as PayPal, eBay and Zynga, in addition to working at a number of startups.

    • Jordan Sweetnam

      VP, Global Product

      Jordan brings rich e-commerce experience building differentiated products that delight customers and drive growth for Walmart eCommerce. Jordan is responsible for building a high-performance UX/product team that Innovate across customer experience, mobile and our technology platform by partnering with all GeC business teams.

    • Srinivasan Venkatesan

      VP, Customer Promise

      Srini and his team of engineers are responsible for inventory, order management and innovating state-of-the-art capabilities for Walmart’s online fulfillment process. The team optimizes Walmart’s fulfillment network to deliver online orders to customers faster. Srini brings in more than 20 years of engineering and entrepreneurial experience in the areas of ecommerce, SaaS Products, advertising, big data and real-time analytics. Srini held leadership positions in a number of companies such as Yahoo, Marketo, Stubhub/EBay.
