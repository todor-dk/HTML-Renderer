    Evolving your Pokémon faster

    If you didn't know, the fastest way to level up your trainer has always been to catch as many low rarity Pokémon, like Pidgey and Caterpie, and evolve as many as you can after using a Magic Egg. By doing so you'll be able to get the most out of the XP bonus from the egg.
    If you force close your app as soon as the animation for evolution starts, you'll be able to squeeze more evolutions in. As long as the sequence has begun, your Pokémon will still evolve, regardless of you closing the app. This may seem like a lot of work, but the amount of extra XP you'll be able to get will definitely add up and be worthwhile.

    Level up your Pokémon extra high

    Like trainers, all Pokémon have levels. Everytime you hit that big Power Up button, you raise your Pokémon by half a level. Officially, the Pokémon's level cap is restricted to 1.5 above the level of the trainer. This means that if your trainer is level 20, your Pokémon are restricted to being 21.5. Luckily for us, there's a way to go between 0.5 and 1.5 higher than officially allowed. By pressing two or three fingers on the Power Up button, when your Pokémon is just 0.5 below its cap, the game will think that it's allowed to give it additional levels. Double Power Ups are easy, but triple can be somewhat difficult.

    Leveling up your Gym fast


    Leveling up your Gym fast
    There's a way to prestige your Gym to level 10 really quickly, and it's called the Bubblestrat. The basic principle is to place a level 2 Pokémon, which has roughly no more than 20CP and has a slow attack, onto the Gym. You then attack it using a level 1 Pokémon, which has a very high attack speed, to kill it before it's able to retaliate. By repeating this process, you'll level up your Gym super quick.
    If you manage to do this properly, you'll be maximizing your prestige gain and XP, without the need of additional items like Revives and Potions.
    Tweaks have happened quite often to the way XP and Prestige is calculated, so if you're planning on using this strategy, it's best to do it as soon as possible before it gets patched.

    Hatch your Eggs when in a vehicle

    Although it's definitely a good thing that Pokémon does everything it can to stop us playing while driving, it, unfortunately, stops passengers from playing also. As soon as your speed increases over 10KM per hour, the game will stop its tracking for walking. This of course means that any distance recorded will not go towards hatching your eggs.
    All of these precautions really are for the best, and if they didn't have them in place they'd probably end up with a lawsuit. However, there are a few ways around it.
    There are a few methods but this is the easier. You should go to the egg screen then hit the home button. Make sure the screen turn off, and don't launch any other applications. Drive for a few minutes, but nothing over 10. Upon opening Pokémon Go again, you should see that all that distance has been counted as walking!
