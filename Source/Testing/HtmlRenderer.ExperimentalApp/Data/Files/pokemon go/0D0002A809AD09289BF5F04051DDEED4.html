I caught some rare Pokemon in the middle of Manhattan, and it's clear why people are obsessed with Pokemon Go

Jacob Shamsian
Jul. 7, 2016, 2:03 PM
989,917
facebook twitter pinterest email copy link

Now you can literally go outside right now with your smartphone and start catching Pokemon.

Pokemon Go is an augmented reality game that lets you walk around, collect Pokeballs, catch Pokemon, and join gyms. It's available for Android and iPhone.

Fortunately, INSIDER's office is located in the Pokemon-rich Flatiron district of New York City. I ventured outside to see what I could find.

1/

Because playing the game involves roaming around, it can be dangerous! Pokemon Go's loading screen doubles as a warning to stay alert of your surroundings.

2/

I started hunting right outside my office. You can see Manhattan's grid layout from the map.

3/

For reference, this is the actual location.

4/

I wanted to go out and find the best, rarest Pokemon in the city. But wait, what was this?

5/

I asked Professor Willow, the game's resident hot dad who answers all your questions. It's a Pokemon Gym! Unfortunately, I couldn't enter until I got up to level 5.

6/

Alright, so what are these things everywhere?

7/

Professor Willow told me they're PokeStops. Their locations correspond to real-life areas, so they basically make you an urban explorer. I went to one by the Lincoln Statue, in Union Square. If you spin the wheel, you get free Poke Balls and other items. Poke Balls are used to capture Pokemon.

8/

The game felt like stepping into another dimension. Things aren't quite right. Here's a Pokestop for "Circle Building," nearby.

9/

...but in real life, there's nothing there. (It looks like the circle was part of a sculpture exhibit from 2013.)

10/

But I'm here to become a Pokemon Master, so I walk around a bit trying to figure out how to find Pokemon. Suddenly, an Eevee appeared! When you encounter a Pokemon, the game's interface changes, and you have to point your camera to the spot where it is. Other people continued to walk around in the background, unaware that I found an incredibly rare Pokemon.

11/

Unlike the Pokemon GameBoy games, you don't battle Pokemon in the wild, you just catch them. I threw one of my Poke Balls at the Eevee.

12/

Got it!

13/

Part of playing Pokemon Go is finding landmarks around the city that you didn't know existed. For instance, I had no idea Union Square had a plaque dedicated to President Jackson's Procession. Interesting!

14/

For whatever reason, the Procession was designated as a magnet for Pokemon, meaning that Pokemon appeared nearby at a higher rate than in other places. I spotted a Doduo!

15/

It's a type of two-headed bird.

16/

I caught it with just one Pokeball.

17/

From the main screen, you can see which Pokemon are nearby. The silhouettes are the ones you haven't encountered yet. Below each silhouette are a few footsteps. The more steps, the farther the Pokemon. I spotted a Pinsir, which is really rare in the other Pokemon games, so I wanted to find it.

18/

Unfortunately, Pokemon Go doesn't give more clues about which direction to move in. But then I saw a Staryu, a water-type Pokemon. That was weird. I wasn't near a pond or lake.

19/

As I got close to the Staryu, I realized â it was right by a sprinkler.

20/

I found the Staryu and caught with just one Pokeball. It was unsettling. It's much harder to catch Pokemon in other games.

21/

And the more I chased after Pokemon, the more I got absorbed into the game. It reminded me of when I was a kid and played the games on my Gameboy instead of listening to whatever my parents were saying.

22/

Being what demographers call a "millennial," I was very good at walking around while looking at my phone and not bumping into anyone. But I was so absorbed in finding the Pinsir that I crossed a street without looking both ways. Luckily, the light said "walk" ... but what if it hadn't?

23/

I was still looking for the Pinsir when I found this Poliwag. Instead of catching it easily, my Pokeballs bounced off it! The Poliwag got angry. Pokemon Go suddenly became harder and more exciting.

24/

After refining my aim with a few more tries, I finally got it.

25/

Unfortunately, at this point, Pokemon Go's servers crashed. The app is surging in popularity right now. I decided to postpone my adventure. Some day, I'll come back and find that Pinsir.
