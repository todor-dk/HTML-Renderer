91LSI/ 92 vert/ 96 1.3l (sold), 92 vert parts car ,91 3/5 89/3/5
 so its safe to assume that you are running "taller" tires than what is on the vehicle tag. So what you should do go oline and find the diameter of the tires that you are currently running and divide that number by the diameter of the tires that are "supposed" to be on the car. you'll prob get a number around 1.04. I think mine came out to 1.036 or something close. That will be your multiplier. So when your speedo thinks your going 50mph your tires are larger traveling a longer distance at the same shaft rpm. so 50 times 1.04 equals 52mph. Your odometer is calculated off the same cable as your speedo so you'll havethe same offset. do this based of the expected tire diameter vs the actual tire diameter and not off your gps and speedo reading. physical measurements are more precise. so the short answer to your question is yes. do the calculations to be more accurate

I drove down the road with my GPS the other day and noticed that when the cars speedometer was on 50, that the GPS would read, 52-52.5mph - which would make the speedometer about 5% off - does this also mean that the odometer readings will be 5% off?
The odometer reading is off by x amount, not exactly like your speedometer is at given speeds.
i.e. - 2 tires, one oem, one slightly taller.
155/80-13 - 60 mph (accurate, in real life this never happens even with tires of the same size/brand) -
Revs per mile 886.7

185/70-13 - When speedometer reads 60mph (96.6km/h) actual speed will be 61.2mph (98.5km/h): 2% faster -
Revs per mile 868.7

In other words, the speedo will be off by a % based on the speed you are driving, the odometer will always be off per mile driven.

Since your relying on gps for the speed, you may also try to rely on it for distance, comparing that to the odometer readings to find out how far its off.

This is the key for odometer testing. GPS units are pretty good at speed, but not necessarily at distance, sometimes they measure as the crow flies and roads don't go like that.

Speedometer and odometer error generally go hand in hand according to tire size, but neither one is is perfect, they could have been off a bit from the factory. For that matter tire sizes are just a guesstimate, four 155/80-13's from four different manufacturers will be four different diameters.
