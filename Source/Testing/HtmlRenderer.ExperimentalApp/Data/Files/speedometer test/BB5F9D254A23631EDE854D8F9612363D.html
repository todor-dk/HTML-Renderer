Re: Bachrus Model Train Speedometer
Author: RioGrandeFan

I have a Bachrus with the JMRI input and for me it has been a so-so experience. I found that the roller that turns the speed sensor is harder to turn than the other rollers and sometimes my locomotives would just slide against that roller or would slide and then turn it and then slide again which gives very inaccurate results. I even took the unit apart and checked for why it wasn't easy to turn and found some flash on plastic parts but it still wouldn't turn easy. I then put a couple drops of oil on the bearing surface, results were better but still not great. Overall I have grown to not be able to trust the accuracy of the Bachrus and always go back to the Accutrack speedometer tunnel which is mobile and very accurate.

The Bachrus rollers are wonderful though. I'm not totally sold on the speedometer though.

I'm also familiar with the Boulder Creek Engineering speedometer and it is also a big winner, if you want to install it permanently in your layout. Nothing at all wrong with that if that is what you are looking for.

Lee Ryan - Rio Grande Fan
