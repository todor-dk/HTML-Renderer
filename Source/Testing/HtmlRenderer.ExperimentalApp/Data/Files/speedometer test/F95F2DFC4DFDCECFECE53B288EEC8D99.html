Part Type: Gauge Sending Units

Product Line: AutoMeter Programmable Electronic Speedometer Senders

Summit Racing Part Number: ATM-5291

UPC: 046074052910

Sending Unit: Electrical

Sending Unit Thread Size: 7/8-18 in.

Thread Adapter Fittings Included: Yes

Quantity: Sold individually.

Notes: Hall effect style sender, 16 pulse per revolution with pass-thru drive for cruise control. Adapts most GM and Chrysler transmissions with speedometer cable output. This sending unit is designed for use with Auto Meter speedometers, and will not work with OEM speedometers under any circumstances.

In-Store Pickup: Choose In-store pick-up (OH, GA, NV) on our web site.

Use these AutoMeter senders to convert your mechanical speedometer takeoff or electronic sensor-style speedometer output to an electronic pulse output. They are designed for use with custom installations, early model transmission applications, and all AutoMeter electronic speedometers.

Product Related Question
Q:

I am working on a 1986 BlueBird bus with a 5.9 Cummins and (I assume) a 727 transmission. My speedometer adaptor has failed and I am considering a change to electronic. Is this the sending unit I need?

Asked by PETER on June 07, 2017

A:

Thanks for your question. Provided the 727 (or whatever trans is backing up the 5.9) features a 7/8" x 18 thread speedo cable attachment point and a speedo gear in the trans for the drive key to plug into, you will be in good shape with the ATM-5291. Keep in mind we build this part to work with AutoMeter electric speedometers. If you're planning to try to use this part to drive something else, you'll want to verify that the device will accept a 12V square wave signal output of the appropriate frequency. The sensor outputs 16 pulses per drive key revolution, so frequency at particular speeds or pulses per mile will vary by application depending upon speedometer gear tooth count, axle gear ratio, and tire circumference. Alternatively, you may wish to consider the ATM-5289 GPS speed sensor which provides a configurable signal frequency output similar to the above, but utilizes satellite positioning data to calculate vehicle speed, meaning you don't have to interface with the vehicle drivetrain at all, just mount the included antenna with a clear view of the sky.

AutoMeter Answer - June 07, 2017

Q:

Will this work with a 4L80e?

Asked by DAN on May 07, 2017

A:

Thanks for your question. To the best of our knowledge, the 4L80E transmission was not offered with a 7/8 x 18 thread provision to drive a mechanical speedometer cable (which is the port required to connect the ATM-5291). If you are looking to power an electric AutoMeter speedometer from the 4L80E trans, you should be able to utilize the existing factory 2 wire VSS in the transmission case. If that is not available or non-functional and you are unable to find a replacement, or wish to avoid calibration issues related to driveline corrections, you may wish to take a look at our GPS speed sensor, ATM-5289.

AutoMeter Answer - May 08, 2017

Q:

I have a 1991 Chevrolet s10 with electronic speedo and originally had a 2.5 4 cylinder standard trans. now i have a 350 and a 700r4 and wanted to know if this product would work for my application?

Asked by ROBERT on March 17, 2017

A:

Thanks for your question. Unfortunately our vehicle database does not specify the OEM speedometer signal requirement for your vehicle. The ATM-5291 is specifically intended to drive AutoMeter electric speedometers when using GM/Chrysler transmissions that have a 5/8" x 18 thread-on speedometer cable port. The following information may be helpful in determining whether it will work for your desired application. ATM-5291 produces a +12V square wave speed signal output that is 16 pulses per drive key revolution. If the signal type is a match for your needs, calibration of the sensor to your speedometer would be achieved through changing the tooth count on the speedometer gear in the transmission, similar in most respects to adjustments for a mechanical speedometer.

AutoMeter Answer - March 20, 2017
