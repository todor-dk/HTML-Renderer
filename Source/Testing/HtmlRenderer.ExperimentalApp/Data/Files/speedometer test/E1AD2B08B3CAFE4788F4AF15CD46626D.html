Buying Guide

 

Looking for a bike speedometer but have no idea what you should focus on when deciding on one model over the next? You’re in the right place. We’ve decided to help you by putting together a buying guide that lets you make the right choice. Whether you’re looking for a mechanical bike computer or a simple wireless alternative, have a look at the following considerations.

 

ANT+, Wired, or Wireless

ANT+ is a technology that was primarily built by Dynastream Innovations. These days, it’s owned by Garmin and is in the public domain, which is why many models use this kind of communications protocol to offer data regarding the heart rate, cadence sensors, or power. If you’re not particularly interested in getting a bike speedometer that features this piece of technology, we recommend opting for a unit that can be used with the Bluetooth on your smartphone or mobile device of your choice so that it can transfer data about the previously mentioned details.

Wired bike computers are slowly becoming obsolete, but they are still reasonable alternatives for individuals who don’t use apps for tracking their data and strictly want to know the miles they’ve biked for and the approximate number of calories they might have burnt during their biking.

 

GPS

Some prospective buyers might, in fact, be looking for a GPS destined for biking. Don’t worry, there’s plenty of fish in the sea as brands such as Magellan, Garmin, and others have gradually worked on making their navigational systems smaller yet almost the same efficient as those that are currently being used in cars. The GPS feature is practically mandatory if you’d like to track your activity with the help of Strava and other apps that can be employed to analyze the stats of your ride.

 

Touch screen

Various bike riders are less interested in the way they interact with the device. For these individuals, the accuracy of the data provided by the unit matters a lot more compared to the interface and whether or not the model comes with touch screen. If you’re not very picky and wouldn’t want to make a lot of stops so that you can customize the menu and other features of your bike computer, perhaps you’d be better off with a simple model that doesn’t come with a touch screen. If, on the other hand, you’re a technology aficionado that appreciates improvements over single-button interfaces, maybe this is the feature you want to get your hands on.

 

Size

Just like smartphones, the size of bicycle speedometers has significantly increased over the years. While this is not necessarily a bad thing as you’ll be able to look at your progress and heart rate from the corner of your eye, the fact of the matter is that a large bike computer is usually harder to install than a smaller one. If this is your cup of tea, you’re likely to have to use a special mount. This might be an inconvenience for some people, but others don’t seem to be bothered by the bulkiness of a complex mounting system.

Another thing worth mentioning regarding size is that bigger models are usually better. For example, if you go for a bike speedometer with a GPS that features a large screen, it has a high chance of being the most feature-packed one designed by the brand. Once you start analyzing your options for a bigger display, you’ll also have to consider whether you prefer color or black and white.

Mount

There have been cases where the bike speedometer doesn’t come with a mounting system, particularly if we’re discussing units that are for sale. This situation might make setting up the model a bit tricky, particularly for beginners. As such, we urge you to read the product description as attentively as possible to find out if this accessory is offered along with the computer.

There are three types of mounts you’ll stumble upon while doing your research. Some models still use traditional clip-in methods but they’re less preferred by certain brands, which offer a quarter turn mount. Finally, an out-front mount seems to be the top choice of bikers, because it makes the speedometer very easy to see. In the end, you should pick the one you’re most comfortable with.
