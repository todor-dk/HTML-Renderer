Select the type of Internet Service you are currently using.
More information
Cable: Broadband Internet access through cable TV type connection and modem (no phone line required).
DSL: Broadband Internet access through a phone line and modem; the phone can be used simultaneously in most cases.
Fiber to the Home: Optical fiber connecting directly to the home or business; typically a high speed connection which may be bundled with phone and television service.
Wireless to the Home: Connects to the provider through a radio wave link to the premises; an outdoor antenna is typically required for "fixed" wireless. Mobile wireless is more similar to cellular telephone service and sometimes requires an “air card.” 3G and 4G wireless are examples.
Satellite: Internet signal is received from a satellite; often used in remote or sparsely populated areas.
Wi-Fi: A type of broadband wireless service available at public locations such as some libraries and cafes.
Dial-up: Internet access through a phone line and modem; ties up the phone from other calls (typically not considered broadband speeds)
