Understand the terms

Mbps means megabits per second. It’s a standard measure of Internet speed. Latency refers to the time it takes for your device to contact the web host and obtain data. Ping refers to a query from your computer or network to determine if there’s a connection.

Internet Speed Guide

Two key components determine your ideal Internet speed: Users and activity. Adding users makes higher speed crucial to support all devices. Media-rich activity – such as gaming, streaming, and video-conferencing – also need speed. Here’s a guide to figure out which high-speed Internet fits best.

Why Internet speed is important

For value

An Internet speed test can determine whether you get what you signed up for. Several factors affect Internet speed. The average speed, though, should remain close to the plan you chose. Underperformance could reveal network issues. Internet service providers advertise speed “up to” a certain level of Mbps. Those numbers reflect performance under ideal conditions.

For media-rich activity

Greater speed makes it easier to stream, game, videoconference, and more. Low Internet speed will cause movies to pause and buffer. Gaming online against other opponents becomes difficult if slow Internet causes latency. Sluggish Internet will cause long pauses in videoconferencing, too. Even social media sites, with video and photos, load easily with fast Internet.

For several devices

Few households connect with only one device these days. Faster Internet can accommodate multiple devices connected at once. The highest Internet speeds can also support streaming by several users on the network. A Wi-Fi router broadcasts an Internet signal for multiple users within a home.
