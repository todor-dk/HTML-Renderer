How QWERTY
was designed?

The QWERTY keyboard was designed in 1868 by Christopher – the man who invented the first successful typewriter. The order of the keys was decided by listing the most common letter combinations and making sure they are not placed next to each other. This was done to avoid jamming problems that often happened when two neighbouring keys were pressed one after another. The QWERTY keyboard is used to this day and is the most widely spread keyboard type out there.

How many words
can you type per minute?

The average person types between 38 and 40 words per minute (WPM), what translates into between 190 and 200 characters per minute (CPM). However, professional typists type a lot faster — on average between 65 and 75 WPM.

What is the meaning
of CPM in typing?

The CPM stands for the number of characters you type per minute, including all the mistakes. “Corrected” scores count only correctly typed words. WPM is just the corrected CPM divided by 5. This is de facto an international standard.

What is the
average typing speed?

An average professional typist types usually in speed of 65 to 75 WPM. More advanced positions require 80 to 95 (usually the minimum required for dispatch positions and other time-sensitive typing jobs). There are also some advanced typists work that requires speeds above 120 WPM.
