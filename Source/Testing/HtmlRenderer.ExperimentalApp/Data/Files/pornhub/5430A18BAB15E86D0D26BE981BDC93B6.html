    Here's How PornHub is Making Porn Work for the Blind

    It's more than just moaning.

    Most Popular
    Mia Khalifa + Instagram
    By Matt Miller
    Jun 17, 2016

    There are 7.125 billion in the world who probably all watch porn. But in an attempt to make the world's favorite pastime more accessible to the 285 million people who are blind or visually impaired, Pornhub.com is introducing "described video." The website has unveiled a new type of porn in which female narrators detail what's happening in the videos along with the typical moans and groans. 

    One, for example, has the narrator explaining the following situation:

    "When he tells her he can't work his tanning bed she bends over and her ass points right up at him."

    Most Popular

    Another says: 

    "The video begins with a small Latina with dirty blonde hair lying face down on a massage table with nothing but a small towel covering her ass."

    And another: 

    "We cut to a POV of a guy looking at a hot teen lying on a bed on her belly."

    With more than 60 million visitors each day, Pornhub vice president Corey Price told the Huffington Post, "We did feel it important to start to include this type of more-accessible content on our platform though, given the relative ambiguity of the concept and the sheer number of people who are visually impaired around the world."

    Currently, the site features 50 descriptions for already-existing videos—including the Kim Kardashian / Ray J sex tape:

    "The video opens on a medium-distance view of Kim K and Ray J kissing on a large bed. Kim is wearing a white bathrobe and Ray has on a white tank top and shorts."

    What a progressive human utopia we live in.
