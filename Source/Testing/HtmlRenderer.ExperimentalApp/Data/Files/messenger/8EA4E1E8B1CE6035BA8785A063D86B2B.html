Definitions

messenger

  mes·sen·ger

Use messenger in a sentence

    1. a person who carries a message or goes on an errand
    2. a person whose work is delivering telegrams, official dispatches, or the like
  2. Archaic a harbinger; forerunner
  3. Naut. a light line tied to a heavier one and used in pulling it as from a ship to a pier

Origin of messenger

Middle English messengere (with unhistoric -n-) from Old French messagier: see message

messenger

noun

  1. One that carries messages or performs errands, as:
    a. A person employed to carry telegrams, letters, or parcels.
    b. A military or official courier.
    c. An envoy to another person, party, or government.
  2. A bearer of news.
  3. A forerunner; a harbinger: the crocus and other messengers of spring.
  4. a. A prophet.
    b. Messenger Islam Muhammad. Used with the.
  5. Nautical A chain or rope used for hauling in a cable. Also called messenger line .

transitive verb

mes·sen·gered, mes·sen·ger·ing, mes·sen·gers
To send by messenger.

Origin of messenger

Middle English messanger from Old French messagier from message message ; see message .

messenger

Noun

(plural messengers)

  1. One who brings messages.
  2. (nautical) A light line with which a heavier line may be hauled e.g. from the deck of a ship to the pier.
  3. The supporting member of an aerial cable (electric power or telephone or data).
Verb

(third-person singular simple present messengers, present participle messengering, simple past and past participle messengered)

  1. To send something by messenger.
    I'll messenger over the signed documents.
Origin

From Old French messagier (French: messager), from message.
