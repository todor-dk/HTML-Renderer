    The typical equation of a line is: y = mx + b where m: slope and where b: y-intercept

    How to get the equation of the line (AB) that passes through A and B ?

    m = (yB - yA) / (xB - xA) you know that: yA = 0 and you know that: xB = 0

    m = - yB/xA recall the equation (1): yB = - 6/xA

    m = 6/xA

    The equation of the line (AB) becomes:

    y = mx + b we've just seen that: m = 6/xA

    y = (6/xA).x + b equation (2)


    To calculate b

    The line (AB) passes through A. The coordinates of this point must verify the equation of the line (AB).

    y = (6/xA).x + b

    b = 0 - (6/xA).xA

    b = - 6/xA

    y = (6/xA).x + b we've just seen that: b = - 6/xA
