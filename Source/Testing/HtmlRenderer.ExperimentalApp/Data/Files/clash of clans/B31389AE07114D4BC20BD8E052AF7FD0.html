When you first play the game, youre able to unlock quite a lot of the initial village defenses and army upgrades in no time, speedily building and improving your village. Its also easier attacking enemy bases at lower levels. Building new structures and upgrading the ones you have doesnt take long either.

This initial rush of upgrades and the ease of attacking enemy players gets the newbie player hooked. As time goes on, it becomes harder to acquire upgrades, as they dont come cheap anymore. Added to that, the time taken to build new structures and upgrade existing ones increases.

Due to more time and work having to be put into progressing in the game, now with more complicated dynamics, getting that same feeling of satisfaction you derived from first playing the game isnt easy.

Youre pulled into a never ending cycle that eventually becomes a grind to upgrade and improve your defenses, lest being attacked and having your resources stolen, as the higher the level you are, the more you cannot afford to lose valuable resources.

The perpetual need to advance, combined with incrementally increasing time and effort needed to progress in the game results in your addiction to the game, leaving you checking your village every half hour.
