English

Etymology

Shortening of pornographic (Australia, New Zealand) or pornography (North America). Attested from mid-20th century.

Pronunciation

  • (Received Pronunciation) IPA(key): /ˈpɔːnəʊ/
  • Rhymes: -ɔː(ɹ)nəʊ

Adjective

porno (not comparable)

  1. Pornographic [from the 1950s]
    That's a porno film.

Synonyms

  • porn

Noun

porno (countable and uncountable, plural pornos)

  1. (uncountable) Pornography.
    • 2004, Martin Amis, Vintage Amis, page 190:
      In any case, porno is littered—porno is heaped—with the deaths of feelings.
  2. (countable) A pornographic film.
    They're going to watch a porno.
    They watched a few pornos.
