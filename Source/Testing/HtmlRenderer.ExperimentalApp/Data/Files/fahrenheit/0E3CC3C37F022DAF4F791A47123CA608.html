Celsius to Fahrenheit Conversion

Celsius is a temperature scale where the freezing point of water is 0 degrees and the boiling point is 100 degrees. Used to known as centigrade until the mid of 20th century. The abbreviation is "oC".

Fahrenheit is a temperature scale where the freezing point of water is 32 fahrenheit degrees and the boiling point is 212 degrees (100 celsius to fahrenheit is 212). It is replaced by celsius scale as part of the metrication process but it is used in United States for all kinds of applications to measure the temperature. The abbreviation is "oF".

The celsius to fahrenheit conversion formula: Fahrenheit = Celsius * 1.8 + 32
It is because, if you compare the freezing and boiling points for each unit, there are 1.8 fahrenheit degrees for one celsius degree, i.e 212 (boiling point for oF) - 32 (freezing point for oF) / 100 (boiling point for oC)= 1.8

For example: 45 degrees celsius to fahrenheit is: 45 oC = 45 * 1.8 + 32 = 113 oF

Tip:

If you are offline or can't access any tool to convert from celsius to fahrenheit, simply multiply the celsius degree by "2" and add 32 (0 celsius degree is 32 fahrenheit). This will give you a very close result to the correct conversion. If you are converting for lower degrees, the result is closer.

Ex: 10 Celsius = 10 * 2 + 32 = 52 Fahrenheit. The correct conversion is 50 fahrenheit, so the difference is only 2 degrees.

Ex: 35 Celsius = 35 * 2 + 32 = 102 Fahrenheit. The correct conversion is 95 fahrenheit, so the difference is about 7 degrees.
