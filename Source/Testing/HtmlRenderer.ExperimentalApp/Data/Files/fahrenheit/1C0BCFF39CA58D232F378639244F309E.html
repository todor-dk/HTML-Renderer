Fahrenheit 451

Ray Bradbury bio sheet Fahrenheit 451 Terms and Definitions
STUDY
PLAY
Where and when was Bradbury born?
Waukegan, Illinois on August 20th, 1920
When did Bradbury begin writing?
When he moved to Los Angeles.
What college did Bradbury go to?
He did not go to college.
After he got married, how many children did he have?
Four
What was the title of his first book and when was it published?
"The Martian Chronicles" 1950
What was his first book about?
It was about Nuclear War and society's longing for a simpler.
When did Bradbury write "Fahrenheit 451"?
1953
What did Bradbury feel about people? What is his view on censorship?
He felt that people had made their lives too complicated and noisy. They watched too much TV, spent little time along, never formulated their own ideas and thoughts, and were constantly bombarded with noise. He is opposed to censorship of any kind and often addresses this theme too in his stores.
Where is "Fahrenheit 451" set in?
It is set in the future about a society that is under total control of the government. Books and the written word are banned and firemen set fires to books and any houses they are found in. Having been a fireman for years, Montag doesn't think much about how things are until he meets this 17 year old neighbor, Clarrise McClellan.
What is the first section of "Fahrenheit 451" titled?
"The Hearth and the Salamander"
What does the salamander symbolize?
The salamander symbolizes the firemen.
What is the mythology of the salamander?
It lived in flames and was unaffected by them.
What type of society is "Fahrenheit 451"
Equiltarian (dystopian) Society ; If book are banned, the smart will become just as dumb as the ignorant.
What is the significance of the title "Fahrenheit 451"?
451 is the temperature at which books or paper burn.
Chronology
A sequential, time order of past events.
Conflict
The struggle that moves the action forward in a work of literature.
What types of conflict are there?
Man vs. Man
Man vs. Nature
Man vs. Himself
Some consider "Man vs. society" a fourth category
Fiction
A literary work that is not true.
Flat/Static Character
A One-dimensional character who lacks diversity and complexity; a character who is either all good or all bad and does not change.
Irony
A perception of inconsistency, sometimes humorous, in which the significance and understanding of a statement/event is changed by its context.
What types of irony are there?
Dramatic Irony - the audience knows more about a character's situation than the character does and knows that the character's understanding is incorrect.
Structural Irony - The use of a naive hero, whose incorrect perceptions differ from the reader's correct ones.
Verbal Irony - A discrepancy between what is said and what is really meant; sarcasm
Science Fiction
A genre of literature that deals with the combination of scientific knowledge and imagination.
Symbol
An object, person, or place that has a meaning in itself and that also stands for something larger than itself usually an idea or concept; some concrete thing which represents an abstraction.
Theme
The central or dominant idea behind the story; the most important aspect that emerges from how the book treats its subject.
Verisimilitude
Use of realistic elements to make literature appear truthful or accurate.
Utopian
Having possibly idea conditions especially of social organization
Dystopian
An imaginary place which is depressingly wretched and whose people lead a fearful existence.
Allusion
A reference to a well known person, place, event, literary work, or work of art.
Paradox
A statement that seems to be contradictory but that actually presents a truth.
