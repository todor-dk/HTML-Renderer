Fahrenheit to Celsius Formula

Temperature is measured in Fahrenheit and Celsius scale. Fahrenheit is a temperature scale used mostly in the USA. The difference between the freezing point and boiling point of water is 180 fahrenheit degress where the boiling point of water is 212 °F and the freezing point of water is 32 °F. This means that one degree on the fahrenheit scale is 1/180th of the difference between the freezing and boiling point of water. On the celsius scale the difference between the ice point and steam point of water is 100 celsius degrees.  At most times, it happens that we are referring the Celsius scale and we get the temperature in Fahrenheit or the case may be vice versa. Solved Examples

Question 1: Convert Fahrenheit to Celsius:
(a) 0o F
(b) 15o F
(c) 40o F.
Solution:
 
The Fahrenheit to Celsius is given by the formula
C = (F - 32) $\times$ $\frac{5}{9}$
  1. (a) 0o F = (0 - 32) $\times$ $\frac{5}{9}$ = - 17.78o C
  2. (b) 15o F = (15 - 32) $\times$ $\frac{5}{9}$ = -9.45o C
  3. (c) 40o F = (40 - 32) $\times$ $\frac{5}{9}$ = 4.45o C.

 

Question 2: Convert the following in to Fahrenheit:
(a) 25o C
(b) 45o C
(c) 100o C.
Solution:
 
To convert the Celsius to Fahrenheit is given by formula F = C $\times$ $\frac{9}{5}$ + 32
25oC = 25 $\times$ $\frac{9}{5}$ + 32
         = 5 $\times$ 9 + 32
         = 77o F
45oC = 45 $\times$ $\frac{9}{5}$ + 32
        = 9 $\times$ 9 + 32
        = 113o F
100oC = 100 $\times$ $\frac{9}{5}$ + 32
          = 20 $\times$ 9 + 32
          = 212o F.
