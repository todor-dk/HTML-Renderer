Fahrenheit Cologne

Almost 20 years after his elder brother, here Fahrenheit Cologne 32. A fragrance for a man in motion, listening to the wind of desire. That is the man of today, seeking for harmony and purity. Openness to the world.

Created by Hedi Slimane (artistic director of Dior), Louise Turner and Francis Demachy (Director of olfactory development at LVMH) Fahrenheit Cologne 32 is defined as a fresh oriental. It opens with distilled orange blossom (neroli essence) that brings freshness, sweetness and spice to the starting note. Yet it remains fairly quiet between vetiver, wooded, elegant, and vanilla which make up the heart and base notes of the fragrance. Fahrenheit 32 wants ice persists even if a certain warmth in its base notes, as a tribute to his predecessor.

Fahrenheit Cologne 32 wants to be distinguished and resolutely distinctive. Daring and powerful, it exploits the contrast. Its composition was a sensory adventure and very exciting imagination. The name alone is a summary of the spirit of the perfume, because it is 32 degrees Fahrenheit (0 Â° Celsius) the water becomes ice, the known becomes unknown.

The fragrance evokes purity, crystalline whiteness, air, light. It is almost poetic. The bottle in turn, has kept the original form of Fahrenheit, but in a glossy white, almost metallic.

The perfume is aimed at the young man, instinctive, daring. Thanks to the fresh scent of orange flowers, the vanilla and vetiver oil. The freshness is combined with a slight oriental and, of course, woody.

Fragrance: Men
Year established: 2007
Olfactory family: Oriental
Olfactory subfamily: woody-fresh
Olfactory pyramid:
-Top notes: fresh notes, Tunisian Orange Blossom
-Heart note: Madagascar Vanilla
-Base note: Haitian Vetiver
