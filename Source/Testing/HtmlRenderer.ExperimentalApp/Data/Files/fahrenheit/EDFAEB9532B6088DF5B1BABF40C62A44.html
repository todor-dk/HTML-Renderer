Fahrenheit to Celsius Formula

The Fahrenheit to Celsius Formula is C = (F 32) / 1.8

More formally, the formula for Fahrenheit to Celsius is written as [C] = ([F] 32) x 5 9. In the next section you can find a F to Celsius example.

Convert Fahrenheit to Celsius

To convert Fahrenheit to Celsius deduct 32 from the temperature in Fahrenheit, then multiply the term with 5 over 9.

For example, to change 100 F to C calculate subtract 32 from 100 to obtain 68. Then multiply 68 by 5 / 9. The result is 37.77 centigrades.

Below you can find some key temperatures relating to Fahrenheit and Celsius:

  • Absolute zero in F = 459.67 F
  • Absolute zero in C = 273.15 C
  • Intersection of Celsius and Fahrenheit scales: 40
  • Human body temperature 37.0 C or 98.6 F on average

On the Fahrenheit scale, the freezing point of water is approximately 32 degrees Fahrenheit (F) and the boiling point is at 212 F, provided standard atmospheric pressure conditions.

On the Celsius scale, the freezing point of water is approximately 0 degrees Celsius (C) and the boiling point is at 100 C, assumed standard atmospheric pressure.

The approximations between degrees Celsius and degrees Fahrenheit are:

F to C: Subtract 30 and halve the resulting temperature
C to F: Double the temperature and add 30
