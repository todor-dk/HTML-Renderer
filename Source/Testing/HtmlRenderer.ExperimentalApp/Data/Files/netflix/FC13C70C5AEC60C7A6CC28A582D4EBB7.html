Netflix

Netflix, Inc. is an American provider of on-demand Internet streaming media available to viewers in North America, South America, the Caribbean, Denmark, Finland, Ireland, the Netherlands, Norway, Sweden, and the United Kingdom. It also offers flat rate DVD-by-mail in the United States, where mailed DVDs are sent via Permit Reply Mail.

The company was established in 1997 and is headquartered in Los Gatos, California. It started its subscription-based digital distribution service in 1999, and by 2009 it was offering a collection of 100,000 titles on DVD and had surpassed 10 million subscribers. It is considered one of the most successful startup companies of all time by market capitalization, revenue, growth, and cultural impact.

Netflix, Inc. is the world's largest online DVD movie rental service, offering over 8 million members access to over 100,000 titles. Their appeal and success are built on providing the most expansive selection of DVDs; an easy way to choose movies; and fast, free delivery.

Netflix offers 5 subscription plans, from $4.99 per month. There are no due dates, no late fees and no shipping fees. Netflix's "no late fees, no due dates" online movie rental model has eliminated the hassle involved in choosing, renting and returning movies.

How does Netflix work? You order any amount of DVDs you desire by adding them onto your list, they will send you the first three that are on your list. As soon as you send back at least one DVD (in a prepaid envelope), they will instantly ship out the next DVD on your list. There is no limit as to how many DVDs you can receive within any month, so you're saving quite a lot of money by renting online with Netflix. You can keep the DVDs as long as you wish and Netflix doesn't put stickers on top of their DVDs, so you don't have to worry about issues that Playstaion 2 owners have to worry about. You can cancel your account at any day, hour, minute or second, so commitment is not an issue. Also, this isn't a DVD club where they will send you unwanted DVDs. Whatever YOU choose from their huge selection of movies, gets sent to you, that's it.

So why rent from Netflix and not the local shop? That's easy.

  • Netflix carries over 100,000 movies while your local shop has about a thousand or so.
  • There are no due dates and no late fees.
  • You pay one monthly fee and rent an unlimited number of DVDs per month. They even pay the shipping costs back and forth.
  • Netflix also offers over 10,000 instant online movie choices If you need assistance with picking a movie, Netflix has movie reviews from their millions of members and Netflix movie critics.
  • No standing at checkout lines on those busy days.
  • They will let you try the service for 2 weeks, FREE!

Yes, Netflix will let you rent their DVDs for 2 weeks, free of charge. Take advantage of this offer as soon as you can.
