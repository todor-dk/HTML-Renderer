How Netflix Works

If you’re looking for details on how Netflix works, then your quest is at an end. I’ll be explaining it all in just a moment, and you’ll no doubt be amazed at how simple the process is. Remember, Netflix is all about making your online movie rental experience as painless as possible.

  1. Signing Up and Creating a Queue – To sign up, you’ll need to enter information like your mailing address (so you can receive your movies) and credit card number (so you can be billed each month). You’ll also need to select a pricing plan, and I’ll go into more detail on that in just a bit. After you’ve taken a few minutes to do the above, it’s then time to start stocking your queue with movies. Basically, you browse through all (or part) of Netflix’s 100,000 plus movies online and click the “Add” button when you find one you want to see. That DVD rental will immediately be placed in your queue. Each day, over 2 million movies are added to Netflix queues.
  2. Movies Are Sent – Based on your pricing plan, you’ll be able to have a certain number of movie rentals at home at any given time. After you return a movie, Netflix will immediately send out the next film at the top of your queue. Don’t worry, you can also move items around in your queue if your ever change your mind. Postage is pre-paid, so sending and receiving Netflix movies doesn’t cost you a penny. You’ll also be glad to know that Netflix has over 58 distribution centers in the United States, which means that 97 percent of their customers will receive their movies one day after they’re sent. In fact, Netflix ships an average of two-million DVDs each and every day.
  3. Watch Movies – When you receive your movies in the mail, it’s then time to watch them. But don’t get in a hurry, as there are no late fees with Netflix. If you want, you can literally keep a movie for months without incurring any extra charges. Just keep in mind that an at-home movie counts against the maximum number of DVD rentals you can have at any given time. If you decide to watch one of the 15,000 films available for instant viewing, you’ll be enjoying it within seconds (and it doesn’t count against your at-home limit).
  4. Return Movies – When you’re finished with a movie, just place it inside the packet it came in, remove the adhesive strip to seal it closed, and drop it in the mail. It’s just that easy. And don’t worry if the post office loses a Netflix movie rental; you won’t be charged for it.
  5. Lather, Rinse, Repeat – That’s all there is to it: get movies, watch movies, and return movies. As long as you’re a Netflix customer, you’ll repeat these same steps over and over.

One of the Netflix plans that’s also available is the “Netflix limited plan”. This Netflix plan only costs $4.99 per month, and it allows a maximum of 2 DVDs each month and two hours of streaming time to your PC. Movies cannot be streamed to your television with this limited plan, and only one movie can be at-home.

You can choose to upgrade or downgrade your Netflix pricing plan at any time via the company’s website. It literally takes two clicks of your mouse, so it really couldn’t get any easier (unless you consider telepathy easier).

Blu-ray on Netflix

If you’ve recently upgraded to Blu-ray, you’ll be glad to know that Netflix also offers over 1,000 Blu-ray titles. When watching a film on a Blu-ray disc, you can expect improved audio and more vibrant picture quality. To take advantage of this option, you’ll need one of the many Blu-ray players available on the market, plus you’ll be required to pay Netflix an extra fee of $8 per month. And if you want your Blu-ray collection to really shine, it’s recommended that you upgrade your television to a high definition model.

Additional Netflix Services

Besides all the features listed above, a number of additional Netflix services are also available. The following section should give you a good idea of what you can expect when you become a Netflix member.

Netflix Ratings

The average member of Netflix has rated around 200 movies, but there’s no upwards limit (for example, I’ve rated over 5,000 films). This is a great way to see which movies are popular and which are gathering dust on the imaginary shelves of Netflix.

Ratings are as follows:

  • 1 star – Hated It
  • 2 stars – Didn’t Like It
  • 3 stars – Liked It
  • 4 stars- Really Liked It
  • 5 stars- Loved It

Movies can be rated in one click of the mouse, so there’s really no reason not to do it. It increases your involvement in the Netflix community, and it might be a big help to anyone considering that movie in the future. In addition, Netflix will automatically make movie recommendations for you based on the ratings you give. Sixty-percent of members make their selections based on these suggestions.

Pros and Cons of Netflix

In closing, let’s review the pros and cons of Netflix. Hopefully, this will sum up nicely all the material presented before (at least that’s the goal).

Netflix Pros

  • Vast number of movie titles available.
  • Most customers receive movies within one day.
  • Continually expanding, with services now available in Canada.
  • Excellent customer service.
  • Numerous pricing plans are available.
  • Movies can also be streamed over your computer, television, or other devices.
  • Blu-ray discs are also available.
  • Gift subscriptions are available for holidays, birthdays, or anytime.

Netflix Cons

  • Video games are not available for rent.
  • Customers must pay an additional $8 to rent Blu-ray discs.
  • When popular DVDs are released, the customer may have to wait days or weeks for the title to become available (although this is becoming less of a problem as Netflix continues to grow).
