Badoo Cult: All You Should Know About The Notorious Gang

  • Post by Iheoma Hendy July 6, 2017
Advertisement

In recent times, Badoo cult has become a name infamous for terrorising residents of Ikorodu area of Lagos State and responsible for incessant attacks on some residents of the area.

The ritual cult group has carried out heinous crimes, killing scores of women and wiped out entire families in some of their operations. In one of their operations, a pregnant woman was killed, while an 8-year-old girl was abducted from a Church vigil, raped and left for dead.

Local residents claim that Badoo is a ritual group and have accused the police, the traditional institution and other highly placed individuals in the affected communities of not doing enough to secure their lives from the gang’s sustained operations. Here’s what you need to know about the deadly cult:

  • The gang’s modus operandi is to smash the heads of their victims with rocks or grinding stones while in some instances, they inflict bodily injury on victims with machetes and rape their female victims.
  • They usually target houses in secluded areas where the cries of their victims would not be heard and then strike in the dead of night when their victims are fast asleep.
  • The group is reported to have killed at least 26 people and injured 14 between June 5, 2016 and June 27, 2017.
  • There are suspicions that traditional rulers and prominent personalities in Ikorodu are behind the cult’s activities.
  • Members of the cult sometimes write to communities informing them of imminent attacks.
  • The cult allegedly has hideouts in some parts of Ogun State.

A top security source once reported that after killing their victims and wiping the blood with a handkerchief, the group sell the handkerchief at a princely sum of N500, 000 to some highly placed Nigerians.

While refuting claims that some monarchs are behind the secret cult group, National Leader of the Oodua Peoples’ Congress, OPC, Gani Adams recently alleged that some highly placed personalities are involved in the Badoo cult activities. In an interview, Gani Adams said:

“For about two and half years, our coordinators in Ijede have been giving us a lot of information about Badoo. We realized that some highly-placed personalities were involved.

“They use vacancy adverts, among others, to lure would-be Badoo members and they put up some telephone numbers. When you call the numbers, they will call you to come to their office and they will give you money for empowerment, like N250,000.

“From what we have gathered, they normally buy the (bloodstained) handkerchiefs for N500,000, N1m or N1.5m.

“A common man cannot afford that money. It is being organised by some prominent people. The Lagos State Government plays partisan politics with the lives and property of people whereas the first duty of government, whether federal, state or local, is to make sure the lives of the citizenry are protected.”

According to reports, residents of Ikorodu now sleep with machetes and whistles as survival strategies against the dreaded Badoo cult. Investigations reveal that virtually every household in Ikorodu now has whistles, machetes and other implements for self-defence. A resident in distress is expected to blow the whistle many times to signal the presence of Badoo cultists.

See Also: Nigerian MC Mistaken As Badoo Cultist Lynched To Death

The menace of the Badoo cult group has forced residents to adopt the ‘jungle-justice’ method. Residents are of the view that the police is not doing enough to secure them and may have resorted to self-help as was recently witnessed in Odungunyan area of Ikorodu.

In Odungunyan, angry residents lynched three suspected members of Badoo, who according to reports, were seen around 2 a.m in an SUV and a big stone, diesel and cutlass were found in the vehicle but the occupants could not explain what the items were for or where they were heading.

However, residents have been advised not to adopt jungle justice in dealing with suspected members of the cult, but allow the police take apprehended suspects into custody for further investigation.

The Lagos state police says it is combating the deadly gang by joining forces with the Rapid Response Squad, Department of State Services, Lagos State Task Force, the Oodua People’s Congress and local vigilante groups. The police say they have raided the dreaded the group’s black spots in Igbobi-Bayeku, Ibeshe, Ijede and Imota, where they arrested over 100 suspected members.

According to the Lagos State public Relations officer, the police is currently profiling the suspects to screen out innocent persons among them.

Moreover, there are reports that the notorious Badoo cult group are planning to infiltrate Ogun State following a revelation by one of the suspected members that the gang has 10 new camps in the state.

In reaction, the Ogun State Commissioner of Police, Ahmed Iliyasu said he has ordered that a letter of request be written to the Lagos command to have access to the Badoo suspect who has the information with a view to clamping down on the fleeing dreaded cult members.

While parading 37 suspects, including five suspected Badoo cultists, at the state headquarters, the CP said the Ogun Command reinforced with community policing, had made tactical deployment and improved on its intelligence network towards taming criminal activities in the state.
