      The Review

      Editor’s Summary

      For the selfie fanatics out there, Badoo is your online dating haven. The site is extremely visually focused, allowing for the upload of pictures and videos, and even goes as far as to block most useful site features until users have uploaded at least one profile picture. However the site has struck some controversy with how it functions, especially how it geolocates users and shows you where exactly (as in with a map and radius icon) and when exactly you may have crossed path with others on the site. Privacy overreactions aside, the online dating platform actually does a phenomenal job at helping you find others who are often in the same vicinity as yourself and doesn’t miss a beat when it comes to staying up to date with the latest trends in social networking, which in this case consist heavily of visual content.
       
      Badoo is most useful as a dating platform for discovering others nearby. The site boasts an impressive, ever-growing number of members and has made multiple appearances on “best dating app” lists. The site is free to join, with a free companion app that also offers a premium version that will set you back $2.99 to download. In both versions, the platform strongly encourages paid in-app or online purchases to get the most out of the experience. So although it’s free, or relatively cheap to download, expect to spend some money on credits if you want to really experience all the dating site has to offer.
       
      There’s no denying the online dating website and app are social in nature, and as so the platform has a more casual hookup vibe to it. Sure you can send her a digital gift, but you’re more likely to vote yay or nay on her profile and see if the two of you match. Perhaps trying to be its own social network, the app also allows you to share other’s profiles on the likes of Instagram, Facebook, and more, and so just as with any mainstream social media site, you’ll want to put a little thought behind what visuals you share. As an alternative to sharing publicly, you can create private photos, albums, and videos -- and that last feature is rather unique in and of itself.
       
      From a user experience perspective, the site and apps are well designed, visually-pleasing and easy to navigate. The signup process is a breeze, and once you’re on the app it takes only a few seconds to figure out what goes where. The layout is straightforward and profiles are colorful, almost playful in nature. Case in point: your “popularity” is displayed with an iPhone battery icon and your superpowers, a paid feature, are shown as on or off with a superman symbol. And, as a visually-focused app should, Badoo does offer the ability to take photos and videos from within the app.
       
      The other interesting aspect is users simply flock to this app. The website is ranked #150 among all sites worldwide and #1 in the relationships category. Desktop and mobile web visits alone are around 130 million per month with visitors coming in from nearly 200 countries. In the Google Play store the Badoo app holds a 4.3 out of 5 star rating based on over 3 million reviews, and has between 100 million to 500 million downloads. Similarly, in the Apple app store both the free and paid apps hold 4.5 out of 5 star ratings.
       
      The more-than-a-decade-old dating platform may not be the newest and shiniest online dating platform on the market, but it’s certainly up to date and continually growing in popularity, with over 335 million registered members. If you’re looking for an online dating app with some fresh faces, or, more importantly, an app that connects you to people you walk by anyways, Badoo has you covered. Just make sure you get yourself some good selfies and a little spare change to get the most out of this online dating experience.

      IN-DEPTH

      Sign-Up Process

      Badoo allows you to register by connecting various social media accounts, such as Facebook or Twitter, or by answering a few simple questions. The question route requires you to select your sexual orientation, your reason for joining (chat, make new friends, or date), and indicate which sexual orientation(s) you are interested in connecting with (guys, girls, or both). Finally you’ll enter your first name, birthday, location, and email address.
       
      Continuing the signup process, you have the option to import friends from your email addresses and/or upload photos of yourself from Facebook, Instagram, Google Plus, or your device. Likewise, if you sign up with a social network, such as Facebook, your most recent profile pictures automatically import to your profile. Additionally, when you signup via the mobile app you have the option to enable geolocation and push notifications.

      Members & Messaging

      Badoo offers a few different options to discover others, but the primary ones are going through “encounters” and browsing people nearby. The main menu, accessible by swiping right on the app, showcases all interactions with others organized into categories of encounters: people nearby, messages, people who liked you, visitors, favorites, and a flowing stream featuring users who have paid to have their profile highlighted.
       
      The main menu also takes you to your profile where you’ll find icons showing your success on the site. Through a superman icon you can see if your super powers are on or off, while through an iPhone battery icon you can see your popularity level. Superpowers, which include the ability to go back and undo “no” votes to profiles and having your messages read first, can be activated for a week to a lifetime with prices ranging from $2.99 to $49.99. Your popularity, on the other hand, is increased by your activity on the site and the level rises as you add more photos. In the profile section you’ll also see any awards you have won, and have the option to add additional information such as smoking and drinking habits and activities and hobbies. You can verify your profile by uploading at least one photo and connecting with a social network or verifying through a phone number. Finally, your profile hosts all your private photos and videos that can be shared with others as desired.
       
      To discover others, browse through “Interactions” or people nearby. Interactions is essentially a “hot or not” liking game in which you see profiles and can then choose to like or dislike them. Other communication options include sending gifts and, a little unconventionally, sharing the profile on your social networks, such as Instagram and Twitter. To use this section, swipe right to move from profile to profile and click the heart icon to show interest or the large X icon to pass.
       
      In the “people nearby” section you see profiles of others you may have “bumped into” or, more accurately, recently passed by on the street. When clicking on a user’s profile picture in this section you are brought to her profile, with an additional map that geolocates when she was nearby you today -- down to the exact minute. From each profile in this section you may tap the star icon to add the profile to your favorites, click the heart icon to like the profile, click the chat icon to begin chatting, or click send a gift to send a virtual gift.
       
      Credits, called coins, are used to enable various features on the site, such as featuring your profile, and are available for purchase in packages of 100 for $1.99, 500 for $7.99, 1,250 for $15.99, or 2,750 for $31.99.

      Key Features

      • Reliable profile verification option
      • Speedy registration process
      • Very detailed geolocation feature shows when and where you may have bumped into a user IRL
      • Gamification features, including super powers and popularity meter, make the site more entertaining
      • Profiles are well organized to highlight important information without being overwhelming
      • Option to connect with users outside of the site on their connected social networks
      • Paid offering to feature your profile allows you to get millions of views quickly
      • Ability to add both photos and videos in both public and private albums

      Safety & Security

      Badoo uses “Reasonable and appropriate security measures to protect and prevent the loss, misuse, and alteration of the information under our control, including your personal information.” The platform uses multiple measures to safeguard member data, including firewalls and the storage of personal information on secured servers. For more about safety and security, please visit the Privacy Policy.

      Noteworthy

      In a peer-reviewed study by Cambridge University in 2009, Badoo received the lowest score for privacy among the 45 social networking sites examined. According to Google's transparency report released in 2014 on the requests for search removals stemming from the "right to be forgotten" ruling, Badoo had the fourth-highest number of URLs removed from Google Search. In February 2016, Badoo acquired LuLu, the mobile app that allows women to anonymously review and rate men.
