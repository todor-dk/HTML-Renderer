Travelling by Rail

The nearest stations are Harrogate and Starbeck. There are links to these stations from both York and Leeds. York is on the main East Coast Line from London King’s Cross to Edinburgh.  Trains leave from both stations at frequent intervals.

 

Travelling by Car

Visitors approaching from the A1M Northbound. Exit the A1 at junction 45, drive through Wetherby and take the A661, signposted to Harrogate. After Wetherby stay on the A661, then across the A658 (Harrogate Southern By-Pass). Pass a pub called The Kestrel on your left. Shortly after the Kestrel, turn right onto Forest Lane, passing a large cemetery on your left. Stay on Forest Lane. Shortly after the level crossing you will see that Forest Lane ends in a T junction with traffic lights. The NYnet offices (80 High Street, Starbeck) are on the left about 50 yards before that junction. The journey from junction 45 to the offices, should take 20 minutes.

Visitors approaching from the A1M Southbound. Exit the A1 at junction 48 (Boroughbridge) and take the A6055 to Knaresborough. At the traffic lights in Knaresborough turn right, taking the A59 towards Harrogate. This takes you over the river, and then past the Harogate Golf Club on your left. Shortly after the Golf Club you will come into Starbeck. At the top of the slight hill you will then see some traffic lights, at which you should turn left onto Forest Lane. The NYnet offices (80 High Street, Starbeck) are on the right of Forest Lane, about 50 yards after the traffic lights junction. The journey from Junction 48 to the NYnet office should take 18 minutes.
