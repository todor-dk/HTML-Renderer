      Hot and humid with storms possible again later today...

      Today's Forecast

      • 91°
      • 69°

      Storms are ongoing this morning but should move east of the Coulee Region by the end of the morning commute. Even if the rain has ended in your area, standing water, mudslides, and rising water along rivers and streams could bring added danger to the morning commute. Please stay alert for changing conditions!

      Most of today will be spent behind a warm front with heat and humidity pooling in the region. Highs are expected to rise into the upper 80s to low 90s today with dewpoints in the 70s. The high humidity will make it feel warmer than the air temperatures with heat index values in the mid 90s to around 100. Skies may clear partially around mid day with the chance of isolated thunderstorms returning in the afternoon and evening as a cold front moves through.

      Clouds will break up a bit after the front moves through with much cooler temperatures expected on Thursday. Many locations won't even make it into the 80s. Sunshine will take control of the area as high pressure moves in Friday through the weekend.  -Stormtracker 19 Meteorologist Madeline Sky

      Sign up for WXOW's improved weather email services

      Sign up here to receive daily weather forecasts and/or severe weather emails from the Storm Tracker 19 weather team. The severe weather email you receive will contain the alert headlines (i.e. Tornado Watch for La Crosse County, WI. in effect from May 19 at 7:12 PM CDT until May 19 at 9:00 PM CDT). Below the headline is a link that says, "Click here for more information". Clicking that link will direct the user to the web page where you will find more specific information.

      Severe Weather Alert emails are issued by county. Even though a user signs up to receive alerts for a specific city, the user will receive an email for any alert issued for the county that contains the user's city. The service is limited to one location per email address.

      Severe weather alert emails are triggered for the following alert types:

      • Flash Flood Warning
      • Tornado Warning
      • Tornado Watch
      • Severe Thunderstorm Warning
      • Blizzard Warning
      • Winter Storm Warning
      • Hurricane Warning
