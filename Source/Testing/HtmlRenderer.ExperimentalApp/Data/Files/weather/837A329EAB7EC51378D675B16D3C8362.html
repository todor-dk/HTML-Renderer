      Updated Wednesday, July 12, 2017

      It remains hot and very humid across eastern Iowa today.  We've got a frontal boundary draped across the area that will lead to the development of rain and storms.  There is a slight risk for severe weather between, with damaging wind and heavy rainfall the main threats.  The timing looks to be between 2 and 9 PM, and there could also be some hail.  

      High temperatures reach the upper 80s to lower 90s.  A Heat Advisory is in effect from Waterloo to Dubuque and areas south until 8 PM.  Heat Index values could be as hot as 105°.  Stay hydrated and limit your time outdoors.

      A few storms may linger tomorrow morning, but we will start to dry out as the front moves away from us. We stay humid, but not as oppressive as the last few days. Highs will be in the 70s and 80s Thursday and Friday with upper 80s returning for the weekend and into next week.

      Storm Track 7 Meteorologist Kyle Kiel

      Sign up for KWWL's updated weather email services

      Sign up here to receive daily weather forecasts and/or severe weather emails from the StormTrack 7 weather team. The severe weather email you receive will contain the alert headlines (i.e. Tornado Watch for Black Hawk County, IA. in effect from June 19 at 7:12 PM CDT until June 19 at 9:00 PM CDT). Below the headline is a link that says, "Click here for more information". Clicking that link will direct the user to the web page where you will find more specific information.

      Severe Weather Alert emails are issued by county. Even though a user signs up to receive alerts for a specific city, the user will receive an email for any alert issued for the county that contains the user's city. The service is limited to one location per email address.

      Severe weather alert emails are triggered for the following alert types:

      • Flash Flood Warning
      • Tornado Warning
      • Tornado Watch
      • Severe Thunderstorm Warning
      • Blizzard Warning
      • Winter Storm Warning
      • Hurricane Warning
      • Hurricane Watch
      • Inland Hurricane Warning
