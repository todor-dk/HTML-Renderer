Seasonably Hot Conditions

From Brian Germ:

Another seasonably hot afternoon is expected for Central Texas today with highs in the mid to upper 90s. The heat index, or feels like temperature, should reach the lower 100s under mostly sunny skies. Expect south winds at 5 to 15 mph. A stray shower or two may be possible to our east near I-45, however rain chances remain low at less than 10%. Skies tonight will be mostly clear with overnight lows in the mid 70s. Winds will be south southeast at 5 to 10 mph.

Very similar conditions will continue for Central Texas both Thursday and Friday as an upper-level ridge of high pressure slowly builds westward. Expect mostly sunny skies, highs in the mid to upper 90s, south winds, a heat index in the lower 100s, and the possibility for a few stray showers to the east near I-45.

As the upper-high gets situated across the western United States this weekend, rain and storm chances are expected to slightly increase over the region. At this time, only a 20% to 30% chance of rain is in the forecast however. Storm complexes in the Central Plains will have the potential to push southward into parts of Central Texas due to northwesterly flow aloft. There still remains a little uncertainty about the extent of coverage however. Any activity that may form over the weekend could contain heavy rainfall, gusty winds, and frequent lightning. Temperatures will likely be a little lower as well due to the added cloud cover and chance for rain. Highs are expected to be in the low to mid 90s with overnight lows remaining in the mid 70s.
